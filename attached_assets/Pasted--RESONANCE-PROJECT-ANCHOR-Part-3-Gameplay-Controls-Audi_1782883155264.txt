Control Philosophy

The game is mobile-first.

Every gameplay mechanic must be fully playable using touch alone.

No keyboard required.

No controller required.

No braille display required.

No screen reader required.

The game provides its own spoken interface while allowing players to choose between the game's speech and their own screen reader where appropriate.

Players may fully customize gestures.

--------------------------------------------------

Touch Controls

Single-finger drag

Turns the player's head while continuously updating spatial audio.

Two-finger drag

Moves the player.

Double tap

Interact.

Double tap and hold

Context interaction.

Long press

Scan surroundings.

Three-finger swipe

Cycle interaction modes.

Three-finger double tap

Repeat Faience's last message.

Two-finger double tap

Pause.

Four-finger tap

Accessibility menu.

These are starting defaults.

Every gesture must be re-bindable.

Reality Integrated Gameplay

Real-world movement may optionally become gameplay.

The real world becomes the controller.

Players may use:

walking

running

manual wheelchair

power wheelchair

guide dog

white cane

indoor treadmill

stationary touch controls

No player should ever be excluded because of disability.

Every movement mechanic must have a stationary alternative.

--------------------------------------------------

Environmental Transformation

Faience narratively transforms the real world.

Examples:

Sidewalk becomes ship corridors.

Gravel becomes wreckage, asteroid fields or alien ruins.

Grass becomes alien vegetation.

Doorways become airlocks.

Bridges become docking gantries.

Turning onto another street may become changing course in space.

Turning physically may rotate an escape pod or spacecraft.

The player remains safely in the real world while mentally exploring the game world.
