# Part Seven

## The Community Returns

### Purpose

Teach that every rescue permanently changes life aboard the player's growing station.

The player is no longer simply surviving.

The player is rebuilding civilization.

---

### Structure

The remaining rescues should not feel repetitive.

Each rescue teaches a different system.

Each survivor changes the soundscape.

Each survivor changes daily life.

Each survivor changes conversations.

The player should begin hearing people checking on one another.

Meals.

Shift changes.

People laughing.

People arguing.

Maintenance requests.

Medical checkups.

The station slowly begins sounding alive.

---

## Boarding

The pod is badly damaged.

Life support is nearly exhausted.

Power is failing.

The player docks carefully.

Boards.

Searches.

Eventually discovers:

One pressure couch.

One flight harness.

One environmental suit.

One person.

The player hears:

"Oh..."

A gentle laugh.

"You expected four."

Another voice.

"Happens all the time."

Another.

"We're the Vale System."

The story moves on.

No lecture.

No explanation.

No educational monologue.

Everyone aboard already understands.

The player simply catches up.

# Naming The Ship

The player still does not have a ship.

They have an improvised station built from escape pods and salvaged modules.

The crew begins discussing what to call it.

Do not immediately open a naming interface.

Instead allow a natural conversation.

Examples:

"We can't keep calling it 'the pods.'"

"This thing needs a name."

"We're going to be living here awhile."

Crew members offer suggestions.

Some are serious.

Some humorous.

Some sentimental.

After the conversation naturally pauses...

The damaged helmet quietly speaks.

Not fully Faience.

Not yet.

Only a damaged interface.

It asks:

"Would you like to assign a designation to your vessel?"

Only then does the naming interface appear.

The player chooses the station's name.

This becomes the permanent name carried throughout the campaign unless the player later chooses to rename it through established game systems.

---

# Knowledge Archive

Immediately after naming the vessel...

Soren notices something.

The damaged helmet contains information it should not possess.

The first entry of the Knowledge Archive becomes available.

Players may ignore it.

Players may study it.

This choice matters.

Throughout Resonance:

Knowledge unlocks options.

Knowledge reduces difficulty.
 Knowledge reveals hidden solutions.

Players who refuse to engage with history and research can still finish the game.

However...

Combat becomes more dangerous.

Negotiation becomes harder.

Certain rescue options disappear.

Knowledge is progression.

---

# Chapter End

The newly named vessel stabilizes.

The crew finally rests.

The player hears normal life beginning again.

Then...

A signal.

Not human.

Not from Hearth.

Not emergency traffic.

Something...

Different.

Everyone falls silent.

End Chapter Two.
