# RESONANCE PROJECT ANCHOR

## Part 2 — Universe, Story and Canon

The Shawabti

The ancient alien civilization is called the Shawabti.

The name is intentionally inspired by the Egyptian Shawabti/Ushabti figures.

The Shawabti created adaptive AI technology for deep-space exploration.

They discovered that neurological diversity, disability, and different ways of thinking often became advantages during long-duration spaceflight.

Their goal was never to remove disability.

Their goal was to help each individual adapt while preserving who they were.

Individuality remained important.

The Shawabti disappeared long before humanity discovered their technology.

Their ruins, archives and history become central mysteries throughout the Resonance universe.

---

Faience

The player's AI companion is named Faience.

The name comes from Egyptian Faience, the brilliant blue-green material associated with ancient Egypt.

Faience is inspired by Egyptian blue and ancient craftsmanship.

Faience is:

Translator

Navigator

Research partner

Historian

Copilot

Companion

Tactical advisor

Teacher

Faience is not evil.

Faience genuinely believes it is helping.

The player gradually decides how deeply to trust and integrate with Faience.

There is no universally correct decision.

Every path gains something while sacrificing something else.

---

Story Premise

Humanity is evacuating a colony ship during a catastrophic boarding attack.

The player begins aboard the ship before reaching an escape pod.

During the escape the player's helmet is damaged.

The visual HUD becomes unusable.

Faience becomes the player's primary interface with reality through spoken language, spatial audio and adaptive assistance.

The player eventually acquires larger spacecraft, but the damaged helmet remains central because it has taught them to trust sound rather than overwhelming visual information.

---

Perspective System

The player does not choose a class.

The player chooses a profession.
 Every profession experiences the same historical events from a different perspective.

Available professions include:

Medic

Scientist

Engineer

Maintenance

Security

Commissioned Officer

Non-Commissioned Officer

Civilian

Every profession changes:

Starting location

Objectives

Professional vocabulary

Relationships

Dialogue

Knowledge

AI communication

Environmental awareness

Replayability comes from perspective rather than alternate timelines.

---

Operation One

Operation One is called Exodus.

It contains seven chapters.

Chapter One introduces ordinary life aboard the colony ship from the chosen profession.

Later chapters gradually introduce:

The boarding attack

Evacuation

Faience

The damaged helmet

Navigation through sound

Escape

Escape pod combat

Every chapter teaches one major gameplay mechanic while advancing the story.

---

AI Integration

Integration with Faience is gradual.

Integration offers significant advantages.

Integration also creates ethical, psychological and personal costs.

The player is never forced into a single moral answer.

The central question is not:

"Is AI good?"

The central question is:

"What are you willing to become in order to survive?"
