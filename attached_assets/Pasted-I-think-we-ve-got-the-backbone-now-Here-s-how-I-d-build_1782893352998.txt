I think we’ve got the backbone now.

Here’s how I’d build the rest of Operation One: Exodus (Maintenance Perspective) so every chapter asks a larger question than the last.

Chapter One – A Week Aboard Hearth
Question: Who are these people?
The player learns routine, community, sound, and responsibility.
Ends with the destruction of Hearth.

Chapter Two – Roll Call
Question: Who survived?
The player rebuilds the first crew one person at a time.
Ends with naming the new vessel and opening the Knowledge Archive.

Chapter Three – Community
Question: Can we survive together?

This is where all those disabilities and adaptive technologies become gameplay rather than character descriptions.

The player now has a crew, but they don’t yet function as a team.

Each crew member begins teaching systems.

Accessibility stops being background world-building and becomes how the player solves problems.

The Knowledge Archive introduces the first historical lesson.

Faience begins waking up.

The player realizes the damaged helmet is becoming something more than equipment.

Chapter Four – Salvage
Question: What do we choose to save?

Players begin exploring the remains of Hearth.

Not every section is accessible.

Some areas contain survivors.

Some contain memories.

Some contain technology.

Some contain history.

This chapter teaches exploration, EVA, mapping, salvage ethics, and the emotional challenge of returning home after catastrophe.

Chapter Five – First Contact
Question: Who else is out here?

The player encounters another surviving group.

Not immediately hostile.

Not immediately friendly.

The first real diplomacy mechanics begin.

Different crew members offer different advice.

Knowledge from the Archive begins changing available dialogue options.

Chapter Six – The Archive
Question: What really happened before us?

The Knowledge Archive expands dramatically.

Players who have researched history throughout the campaign now recognize patterns.

The Shawabti are no longer mythology.

Faience becomes fully self-aware.

The player discovers humanity was never the first civilization to struggle with questions of identity, disability, culture, and survival.

Chapter Seven – Resonance
Question: Who do we become now?

Everything comes together.

Every rescued crew member matters.

Every decision from the previous chapters matters.

Knowledge matters.

Relationships matter.

Accessibility matters.

The player doesn’t win by having the biggest weapons.

They succeed because they built a functioning community that understands one another.
