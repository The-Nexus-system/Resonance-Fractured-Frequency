# Resonance: Fractured Frequency — Design Report

## Chapter design rule (canon, franchise-wide)
- Every chapter must introduce one major gameplay system.
- Every chapter must answer one major narrative question.
- Every chapter must end by asking a larger question than it answered.
- Gameplay progression and narrative progression must grow together.
- The player should feel they are becoming more capable for the same reason the story is becoming more complex.

## Operation One: Exodus — chapter question spine
Every chapter begins with a question and ends by asking a larger one. The progression grows from personal survival to civilization.

- Chapter One — Can I save my home? -> No.
- Chapter Two — Can I find my people? -> Yes.
- Chapter Three — Can we survive together?
- Chapter Four — Can we build something worth protecting?
- Chapter Five — Who were the Shawabti, really?
- Chapter Six — Why did they choose humanity?
- Chapter Seven — What kind of civilization will we become?

## Chapter Two: Roll Call — summary
Premise: Begins continuously from Chapter One (no loading screen, no mission-complete screen). Turns isolation into community. Rescue — not combat — is the focus. People are progression.

Emotional themes: Isolation, Shock, Hope, Responsibility, Community, Adaptation, Persistence.

Parts:
1. The Silence After — survival in a damaged escape pod; sensor-to-audio translation introduced.
2. Roll Call — searching for survivors; uncertainty; first familiar voice (Rowan).
3. Closing the Distance — movement, momentum, patient approach through Hearth's debris.
4. First Dock — velocity/rotation matching, careful docking.
5. Bringing Rowan Home — boarding, stabilizing hazards.
6. The First Home — a crew member is "home" only once they start contributing.
7. The Community Returns — the station begins to sound alive.

Canon rescue order (Maintenance campaign):
1. Rowan Ives — repairs, salvage, fabrication (already aboard)
2. Mara Chen — engineering, power routing, upgrades
3. Doctor Samira Kade — medical triage, crew health
4. Chief Petty Officer Jonah Briggs — organization, mission planning, logistics
5. Security Lead Talia Rusk — combat introduced here for the first time
6. Dr. Soren Qadir — research, the Knowledge Archive
7. Mira Ives — hope; advanced AAC interaction
8. The Vale System — emotional climax; the "four captains" turn out to be one person

Climax and end: the crew names their improvised station (natural conversation, then the damaged helmet offers a naming interface) -> the Knowledge Archive opens (knowledge = progression) -> a non-human, non-Hearth signal arrives and everyone falls silent.

End state: first crew rebuilt, station named, mystery expanded, player now responsible for a community — which Chapter Three inherits.
