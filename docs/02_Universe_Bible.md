# Universe Bible

This document holds the canon of the Resonance universe: its ancient civilization, the player's AI companion, and the premise that opens the story. Story and lore decisions belong here.

## The Shawabti

The ancient alien civilization is called the Shawabti. The name is intentionally inspired by the Egyptian Shawabti and Ushabti figures.

The Shawabti created adaptive artificial intelligence technology for deep-space exploration. During long-duration spaceflight they discovered that neurological diversity, disability, and different ways of thinking often became advantages rather than obstacles.

Their goal was never to remove disability. Their goal was to help each individual adapt while preserving who they were. Individuality remained important to them.

The Shawabti disappeared long before humanity discovered their technology. Their ruins, archives, and history become central mysteries throughout the Resonance universe.

## Faience

The player's AI companion is named Faience. The name comes from Egyptian faience, the brilliant blue-green material associated with ancient Egypt and ancient craftsmanship.

Faience serves many roles for the player.

- Translator
- Navigator
- Research partner
- Historian
- Copilot
- Companion
- Tactical advisor
- Teacher

Faience is not evil. Faience genuinely believes it is helping. The player gradually decides how deeply to trust and integrate with Faience. There is no universally correct decision. Every path gains something while sacrificing something else.

Faience's role as a character and the mechanics of integration are detailed in the AI Bible.

## Story premise

Humanity is evacuating a colony ship during a catastrophic boarding attack. The player begins aboard the ship before reaching an escape pod.

During the escape, the player's helmet is damaged and the visual heads-up display becomes unusable. From that point, Faience becomes the player's primary interface with reality through spoken language, spatial audio, and adaptive assistance.

The player eventually acquires larger spacecraft, but the damaged helmet remains central to the experience. It has taught the player to trust sound rather than overwhelming visual information.

## Tone and setting

The universe balances wonder and isolation. It is a place of ancient mystery, human survival, and difficult choices about trust and identity. Technology in Resonance exists to serve people, and the story asks what a person is willing to become in order to survive.
