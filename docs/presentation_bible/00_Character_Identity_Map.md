# Character Identity Map

This document is the locked, canonical identity assignment for the recurring cast of Resonance. It records each recurring character's profession, ethnicity, gender, pronouns, and disability so that every profile, description, animation, and line of dialogue in the Player Identity and Character Presentation Bible rests on the same foundation. This map does not create new rules. It applies the canon rules in the Canon Rules document, which remains the authority; where any wording here appears to differ from a canon rule, the canon rule governs.

The map satisfies the representation rules exactly. The eight recurring human bodies fill the eight ethnic slots, and every gender identity named in the canon rules appears at least once. The eight recurring human characters also correspond one to each of the eight professions, so the cast represents the whole crew as well as the whole of the ship's community. Faience is a Shawabti artificial intelligence and stands outside the human rules for ethnicity, gender, and pronouns.

Every recurring character is a complete person first. The identity facts below describe one meaningful aspect of each character; they never replace personality, relationships, values, strengths, flaws, goals, and growth, which the profiles in this bible carry in full.

## The Vale System

The Vale System is one shared body inhabited by four recurring members who front in turn. The body is constant and the presentation changes with whoever is fronting, as the Canon Rules describe. The whole system shares one ethnicity and one medical reality.

- Profession: Command, in the Commissioned Officer track.
- Ethnicity: Native mixed-heritage, shared by the one body.
- Disability: dissociative identity disorder; the four members share one body and the single office of Captain.

The four members each hold a distinct gender and a distinct set of pronouns, which crew, friends, and family use as an ordinary part of daily life.

### Elian of the Vale System

Elian meets ordinary mornings and community leadership.

- Gender: genderfluid.
- Pronouns: determined by Elian's current identity and presentation.

### Naomi of the Vale System

Naomi meets emergencies, operations, and coordination.

- Gender: non-binary.
- Pronouns: they and them.

### Gideon of the Vale System

Gideon meets strategy, navigation, and failing systems.

- Gender: agender.
- Pronouns: they and them.

### Iris of the Vale System

Iris meets civilian planning and diplomacy.

- Gender: demigirl.
- Pronouns: she and they.

## Mara Chen

Mara Chen is the chief engineering systems specialist, known for a sure, listening gait and for describing systems as sounds.

- Profession: Engineering, in the Engineer track.
- Ethnicity: Asian.
- Gender: cisgender woman.
- Pronouns: she and her.
- Disability: blind; navigates by sound, vibration, and touch, and uses an engineering helmet whose navigation faceplate turns visual data into audio and tactile cues.

## Doctor Samira Kade

Samira Kade is the senior medical officer, whose presence in a bay is the soft mechanical whisper of her sleeve projector.

- Profession: Medical, in the Medic track.
- Ethnicity: White mixed-heritage.
- Gender: transgender woman.
- Pronouns: she and her.
- Disability: Deaf; reads a room by sight and vibration and communicates through a sleeve projector that shows sign or live text.

## Rowan Ives

Rowan Ives is the maintenance supervisor, who knows the hidden body of the ship and has redesigned his work around his condition.

- Profession: Maintenance.
- Ethnicity: Black.
- Gender: cisgender man.
- Pronouns: he and him.
- Disability: chronic pain and arthritis.

## Mira Ives

Mira Ives is a habitation-deck child and the daughter of Rowan Ives, often the first to hear when something aboard the ship is wrong.

- Profession: Civilian.
- Ethnicity: Black mixed-heritage.
- Gender: genderfluid, as a child still coming to understand this for themselves.
- Pronouns: she and they, subject to development as the character grows.
- Disability: autistic and with attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder; communicates through a visible neural-interface augmentative and alternative communication system.

## Chief Petty Officer Jonah Briggs

Jonah Briggs is the senior non-commissioned officer of logistics and operations, the calm voice that reads out priorities.

- Profession: Logistics and Operations, in the Non-Commissioned Officer track.
- Ethnicity: White.
- Gender: transgender man.
- Pronouns: he and him.
- Disability: epilepsy; the ship's seizure-safe lighting is standard design.

## Security Lead Talia Rusk

Talia Rusk is the security lead, steady under threat and inclined to put others ahead of herself.

- Profession: Security.
- Ethnicity: Native.
- Gender: Two-Spirit.
- Pronouns: she and her.
- Disability: lower-limb amputee; uses a prosthetic limb.

## Doctor Soren Qadir

Soren Qadir is a senior research scientist, a persistent investigator who refuses to leave until his team is away.

- Profession: Research, in the Scientist track.
- Ethnicity: Asian mixed-heritage.
- Gender: demiboy.
- Pronouns: he and they.
- Disability: wheelchair user; a repulsor-assisted chair with a manual backup.

## Faience

Faience is a Shawabti artificial intelligence and is not human. Faience stands outside human concepts of race, ethnicity, and gender, and none are assigned. Faience is silent through Operation One and first stirs, wordless, in a damaged pod on Attack Day.

## Open Development Items

Two items are deliberately left open and must be developed with care before they are treated as settled.

- Talia Rusk is the Two-Spirit character, and Two-Spirit is a culturally specific Indigenous identity. Her nation, culture, and community are to be developed thoughtfully, ideally with Indigenous cultural consultation, and are not invented casually. Until then, Talia is written with respect for that specificity and without a placeholder nation.
- Mira Ives is a child who is genderfluid and uses she and they. Because Mira is still growing into her own understanding, her pronouns and the way she expresses her gender are open to development as the character and the story mature.
