# Future Expansion

This document defines how Resonance: Fractured Frequency grows its cast and its world over time without losing its identity. It is written for writers, narrative designers, artists, animators, audio designers, accessibility engineers, and producers who are considering new recurring characters, new species, or new civilisations across future Operations. Its purpose is to make expansion deliberate, consistent, and true to the promises the game already keeps, so that growth strengthens the world rather than diluting it.

This document rests on two higher authorities and never contradicts them. The Canon Rules document is the canon authority. The Character Identity Map is the locked source for each recurring character's profession, ethnicity, gender, pronouns, and disability. Where any guidance here appears to differ from those documents, those documents govern. This file defines process and craft standards for expansion; it does not invent character biography, ship history, place names, species, civilisations, or new game systems as settled canon.

## Foundational Principles of Future Expansion

Expansion in Resonance follows the same pillars as everything else: audio is gameplay, accessibility is foundational, recognition emerges through observation, and representation is character-first and never tokenistic. Every new character, species, or civilisation must serve these pillars, and any addition that cannot must be reconsidered.

### Expansion Serves the Existing Promises

- New content upholds every canon rule already in force and never weakens an existing promise to players.
- Growth honours the canon that community is progression, so new characters deepen the community rather than merely adding headcount.
- Every addition must have a reason to exist beyond novelty, consistent with the rule that no character exists solely to satisfy a requirement.

### Expansion Is Character-First and Non-Tokenistic

- A new recurring character is designed as a complete person first, with history, personality, strengths, flaws, relationships, goals, fears, profession, and growth.
- Representation informs a new character's identity but never replaces it, and no new character is added to check a demographic box.
- The breadth of humanity continues to be reflected as an ordinary part of civilisation, with no identity treated as the default.

### Expansion Preserves Accessibility as Foundation

- Every new character, species, and civilisation is presented through audio-first and blind-friendly means, not sight alone, from the moment they enter the world.
- New content is playable through multiple input and output methods and is configured by experience rather than diagnosis, consistent with the Accessibility Bible.
- Accessibility is designed into new content from the start and is never retrofitted after the fact.

### Expansion Preserves Recognition Through Observation

- New characters become recognisable through voice, movement, professional habit, and relationships rather than through interface labels.
- New content maintains the acoustic identity discipline, so new locations and communities are recognisable by sound.
- Recognition of any new presence emerges through lived experience and observation, consistent with the canon rules.

## Adding a New Recurring Character

A new recurring character is a significant, lasting addition to the community and is developed with the same depth as the established cast. This section defines the process from concept to a presentation profile ready for canonisation.

### New Recurring Character Concept Standards

- A new character begins with a complete-person concept covering personality, values, relationships, goals, fears, strengths, flaws, and intended growth, before any identity facts are locked.
- The concept establishes why this person belongs in the community and what they uniquely contribute, consistent with no crew member becoming obsolete.
- The concept identifies which of the eight professions the character belongs to, from Medic, Scientist, Engineer, Maintenance, Security, Commissioned Officer, Non-Commissioned Officer, and Civilian, and what that profession uniquely notices and is responsible for.

### New Recurring Character Identity Standards

- A new character's ethnicity, gender, pronouns, and disability are chosen to deepen authentic representation and are recorded in the Character Identity Map through the proper decision process before the character is treated as settled.
- New characters continue the broad, global representation the canon requires, and any new gender or communication experience is portrayed authentically as an ordinary part of the world.
- Pronouns reflect current presentation and may change over time, and the world uses a character's current pronouns as an ordinary part of daily life.

### New Recurring Character Presentation Profile Standards

- Every new recurring character receives a complete presentation profile covering physical appearance, clothing, and equipment; accessibility devices; audio identity, voice, accent, and speech rhythm; movement and environmental sound; a blind-friendly, audio-first description; professional habits; civilian presentation; and emergency presentation.
- The new character receives a stable character identifier and asset organisation consistent with the technical standards, so their content is findable and safe to change.
- The new character receives authored idle, locomotion, professional, and emotional animation with audio and, where relevant, tactile equivalents, and a portrait paired with an audio-first description that follows the belonging rule.

### New Recurring Character Belonging Standards

- A new rescued character is not home immediately; they become home only after they settle, begin contributing, and change the environment around them, and only then does their portrait illuminate.
- The new character's arrival changes the station's soundscape, so the community is heard to grow before it is seen to grow.
- The new character remains mechanically and emotionally relevant across the campaign, with meaningful opportunities to contribute according to their expertise.

### New Recurring Character Cultural Care Standards

- Cultural specificity for a new character is developed thoughtfully and, where it concerns a living culture, ideally with consultation from that community, and is never invented casually.
- A new character whose culture is not yet developed is written with respect for that specificity and without a placeholder culture, following the example set for Talia Rusk, whose nation, culture, and community remain to be developed with Indigenous cultural consultation.
- A new character who is a child is given room to grow, with identity aspects that are still forming treated with a light, respectful, non-fixed touch, following the example set for Mira Ives.

## Preserving Consistency

Consistency is what lets a player's hard-won recognition survive across chapters and Operations. This section defines how expansion protects the established cast, the Vale System, Faience, and the world's rules.

### Consistency With the Character Identity Map

- Every addition is checked against the Character Identity Map, which is the locked source for the established cast, and no addition may contradict it.
- Changes to an existing character's presentation over time are expressed as authored growth and ageing rather than as contradictions of established identity facts.
- Any proposed change to a locked identity fact requires the formal decision process and is recorded, rather than being made silently in a new asset.

### Consistency for the Vale System

- The Vale System remains one shared body inhabited by four members who front in turn, and expansion never presents them as four separate people and never as a single Captain Mara Vale.
- New content keeps the body constant while presentation changes with the fronting member, and keeps personal identifiers updating automatically as ordinary ship operations.
- New situations that call for the Vale System respect fronting as gameplay, so a member comes forward because their knowledge, temperament, or expertise fits the moment, and recognition emerges through observation.

### Consistency for Faience

- Faience remains a Shawabti artificial intelligence outside human concepts of race, ethnicity, and gender, and expansion never assigns Faience a human identity.
- Faience's established arc, silent through Operation One and first stirring wordlessly in a damaged pod on Attack Day, is respected, and any later development of Faience is defined through the proper process rather than assumed.
- New content involving Faience honours the philosophy that the artificial intelligence is a character and that technology exists to serve people.

### Consistency of Recognition Signals

- New content preserves each established character's movement signature, voice, and professional habits so returning players keep their recognition.
- New locations and communities receive their own acoustic identities so recognition by sound continues to work as the world grows.
- Recognition is never shifted onto interface labels as a shortcut when expanding the cast or the world.

### Consistency of Accessibility and Audio Standards

- New content pairs every visual asset with its accessible counterparts, including audio equivalents where relevant, tactile equivalents for gameplay-relevant cues, and authored blind-friendly descriptions.
- New audio routes to the correct independent volume channels and never makes critical information depend on a single sensory channel.
- New content honours seizure-safe presentation consistent with the ship's standard seizure-safe design.

## Adding Future Species

Future species are a possible avenue of expansion and are approached with great care, because a species is a large addition to the world. This section gives forward-looking guidance and does not assert that any particular species exists in canon.

### Future Species Design Standards

- A future species is designed as a coherent people with its own history, values, and ways of living, and never as a single-trait stand-in or a metaphor that flattens real human groups.
- A future species is introduced through the same rhythm the canon requires of expeditions, so its introduction expands understanding, answers one question, and raises new ones.
- Any new species is defined through the proper decision process and recorded in the appropriate world documents rather than invented as settled canon in a presentation asset.

### Future Species Recognition and Audio Standards

- A future species receives its own recognisable audio identity, including characteristic voice, movement, and environmental sound, so it can be recognised through observation.
- A future species has authored audio-first and blind-friendly presentation from the start, so players who do not see can perceive the species as fully as players who do.
- Where a species communicates differently from humans, that communication is treated as valid and is made accessible through the game's output methods, consistent with communication diversity being ordinary.

### Future Species Accessibility and Presentation Standards

- A future species is presented through multiple sensory channels, with tactile and audio equivalents for any gameplay-relevant or safety-relevant cues it introduces.
- A future species that has bodies unlike humans still receives dignified, non-tokenistic presentation, and any assistive or adaptive technology it uses is treated as ordinary standard equipment within its own culture.
- A future species is named and organised in assets according to the technical standards, with a stable identifier that does not misuse human ethnicity or gender tokens.

### Future Species Ethics Standards

- A future species is never designed so that its traits map onto and stereotype a real human ethnicity, disability, gender, or culture.
- A future species is portrayed with the same character-first respect as the human cast, so individuals within a species are complete persons rather than interchangeable representatives.
- Sensitive design questions about a future species are raised for documented decision rather than resolved through casual invention.

## Adding Future Civilisations

Future civilisations are another possible avenue of expansion, extending the world's history and diplomacy. This section gives forward-looking guidance and does not assert that any particular civilisation exists in canon.

### Future Civilisation Design Standards

- A future civilisation is designed with its own coherent history, values, social structure, and relationships to other peoples, and is introduced so that it expands understanding rather than merely adding scenery.
- A future civilisation is defined through the proper decision process and recorded in the appropriate world documents, and specific place names and histories are not invented as settled canon in a presentation asset.
- A future civilisation respects the canon that knowledge must always change something, so encountering it changes dialogue, exploration, relationships, or understanding rather than existing as inert lore.

### Future Civilisation Acoustic Identity Standards

- A future civilisation's places receive distinct acoustic identities so the player can eventually recognise where they are by sound before dialogue confirms it.
- A future civilisation's soundscape is authored as a first-class part of its presentation, consistent with sound as progression and audio as gameplay.
- Silence within a future civilisation's spaces is used deliberately as a storytelling tool and never as the result of missing audio.

### Future Civilisation Accessibility and Diplomacy Standards

- A future civilisation is presented through audio-first and blind-friendly means, and any diplomacy, trade, or conflict it introduces is playable through multiple input and output methods.
- A future civilisation's forms of communication are treated as valid and made accessible, and translation or interpretation is presented as an ordinary part of contact.
- Any hazards, urgency, or navigation introduced by a future civilisation carry tactile and audio equivalents so no critical information depends on sight or sound alone.

### Future Civilisation Ethics Standards

- A future civilisation is never a thin allegory that stereotypes a real human culture, and its people are portrayed as complete persons rather than symbols.
- A future civilisation that echoes real historical dynamics is handled with care and, where it touches living cultures, with appropriate consultation.
- Cultural elements are developed thoughtfully rather than invented casually, following the same discipline the project applies to undeveloped areas of the existing cast.

## Representation Review Before Canonising a Character

Before any new recurring character is treated as settled canon, they pass a representation review grounded in the canon rules. This review is a gate, not a formality, and a character that does not pass is revised before canonisation. The review is expressed as a checklist so it can be applied consistently.

### Complete-Person Review Criteria

- The character has a fully developed personality, history, relationships, goals, fears, strengths, flaws, and intended growth, and is not defined by any single identity fact.
- The character exists for reasons beyond satisfying a representation, accessibility, gameplay, or narrative requirement, consistent with every recurring character being a complete person.
- Representation informs the character's identity without replacing it.

### Canon Alignment Review Criteria

- The character does not contradict any rule in the Canon Rules document or any locked fact in the Character Identity Map.
- If the character is proposed as part of the Vale System, they are handled as a member of one shared body, never as a separate person and never as a single Captain Mara Vale, and the addition respects that the four established members share one body and one office.
- The character does not alter the existence, history, identity, relationships, or appearance of any established recurring character, and the player still never replaces a canonical character.

### Representation Integrity Review Criteria

- The character continues the broad, global representation the canon requires and does not treat any race, ethnicity, disability, communication style, or gender identity as the default.
- Any gender identity the character holds is portrayed authentically, current pronouns are recorded and respected as ordinary, and identities that can coexist are handled consistent with the canon's guidance on gender.
- The character is free of tokenism, and their identity facts are recorded in the Character Identity Map through the decision process before canonisation.

### Disability and Communication Review Criteria

- Any disability the character has shapes their perspective, problem-solving, communication, and relationships without defining their entire identity.
- Any assistive equipment is treated as standard equipment, animated and presented with dignity, and never framed as a burden or spectacle.
- Any communication method the character uses is treated as equally valid and is made fully accessible through the game's output methods.

### Accessibility and Presentation Review Criteria

- The character has a complete presentation profile, a stable identifier and asset organisation, authored animation with audio and tactile equivalents where relevant, and a portrait paired with an audio-first, blind-friendly description.
- The character's presentation makes no critical information depend on a single sensory channel and honours seizure-safe design.
- The character is recognisable through observation via voice, movement, professional habit, and relationships rather than through interface labels.

### Cultural Care Review Criteria

- Cultural specificity is developed thoughtfully and, where it concerns a living culture, with appropriate consultation, and is never invented casually.
- Where a character's culture is not yet developed, it is left open with respect rather than filled with a placeholder, following the treatment of Talia Rusk, whose nation, culture, and community remain to be developed with Indigenous cultural consultation.
- Where a character is still growing into their own understanding of an identity, that aspect is treated with a light, respectful, non-fixed touch, following the treatment of Mira Ives, whose pronouns and gender expression remain open to development.

### Community and Progression Review Criteria

- The character has a genuine path to belonging, so their portrait illuminates only after they settle, contribute, and change their environment, and their arrival changes the station's soundscape.
- The character remains mechanically and emotionally relevant across the campaign and does not become obsolete.
- The character teaches and keeps learning, carries expertise the player does not have, and strengthens the community as progression.

### Canonisation Decision Criteria

- The character passes every criterion above, or the specific open items are explicitly and respectfully left open in the Character Identity Map rather than invented.
- The character's identity facts and presentation are recorded through the proper decision process, and the change is logged so its reason and date are preserved.
- Any new uncertainties raised by the character are added to the project's open questions rather than left unstated.
