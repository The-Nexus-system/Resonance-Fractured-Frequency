# Playtesting Bible

This document defines how Resonance is playtested. Accessibility testing is treated as a first-class development discipline, equal to programming, audio, and design. A feature is not finished until it has been validated with the players who will actually use it.

## Playtesting as a first-class discipline

Accessibility is the foundation of Resonance, so testing accessibility is not an afterthought or a final checklist. It is planned, resourced, and scheduled alongside every other discipline. Real players from the groups below should be involved throughout development, not only at the end.

## Player groups to test with

Testing must include players across a wide range of lived experiences.

- Blind players
- Low-vision players
- DeafBlind players
- Deaf players
- Hearing-aid users
- Cochlear implant users
- White cane users
- Guide dog teams
- Manual wheelchair users
- Power wheelchair users
- Neurodivergent players

## Testing dimensions

Beyond player groups, testing must cover experiences and conditions that cut across many players.

- Cognitive accessibility
- Motion sensitivity
- Fatigue
- Multiplayer accessibility
- Mobile-only players
- Touch-only players

## What every test must verify

- Every important action can be reached and performed through more than one input method.
- Important information is never conveyed by sound alone.
- Captions, braille, speech, haptics, and the tactile language each carry the information they are responsible for.
- Reality-integrated navigation always prioritises real-world safety and can be replayed on demand.
- Timed actions are removed or greatly extended when no auditory input is selected in single-player.
- Accessibility settings are individual in multiplayer and never change another player's experience.
- Gestures and bindings are fully customisable, with no single mandatory gesture.

## How testing informs the documents

When playtesting reveals that a design must change, the change is made by editing the relevant living document and recording it in the Decision Log. Playtesting findings that are still being explored may live in `design_notes/` until they are ready to become canon.
