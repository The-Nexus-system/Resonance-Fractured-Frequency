# Discovery Bible

This document defines curiosity and how discovery works across the Resonance franchise. It explains what discovery is, how it is expressed through every sense, how it is earned rather than handed over, and how each thing a player learns changes the world, the conversation, and the campaigns that follow. Discovery is the engine of Resonance, and this bible governs how that engine behaves so that curiosity is always rewarded and knowledge always matters.

## The nature of discovery

Discovery in Resonance is the act of understanding something that was already true. The world does not wait to be authored around the player; it exists first, and the player uncovers it. Curiosity is the central verb of the franchise, and the game is designed so that paying attention is always worthwhile and always safe to attempt.

Discovery is never trivia and never a collectible for its own sake. Consistent with the canon that knowledge must always change something, every meaningful discovery alters at least one part of the game, whether dialogue, exploration, engineering, medicine, leadership, diplomacy, accessibility, research, relationships, community life, or the player's own understanding. If a discovery would change nothing, it is not built.

Discovery is also honest. The universe distinguishes objective history, meaning what actually happened, from historical knowledge, meaning what a people currently understand. Understanding grows by gathering more evidence, and it is corrected through further discovery rather than through retconning the past. Earlier honest observations are never rewritten; they are deepened.

- Discovery reveals what was already true rather than inventing it on demand
- Discovery is available to every player through multiple senses, never one sense alone
- Discovery is optional in most cases yet consequential wherever it occurs
- Discovery corrects understanding through new evidence, never by erasing earlier records

## Principles of curiosity

Curiosity is protected by design. A player should never feel punished for looking closely, wandering off the obvious path, asking one more question, or returning to a place they thought they already understood. The reward for curiosity is understanding, and understanding is the most valuable currency in Resonance.

These principles hold across every kind of discovery described in this document.

- Attention is rewarded, and careful observation reveals more than haste
- Nothing important is ever locked behind a single sense, so sight, hearing, and touch each offer a genuine route to the same understanding
- Discovery expands questions faster than it settles them, so every answer opens a larger one
- Deliberate mysteries stay mysteries, and the game describes how revelation happens rather than delivering conclusions the story has not reached
- Revisiting is encouraged, because a place, a log, or a person can offer new meaning once the player knows more

## Environmental storytelling

The environment is the primary storyteller in Resonance, and it carries meaning that would otherwise be delivered through exposition. A room describes the people who lived in it, a corridor remembers the emergency that passed through it, and a workshop records the trade practised there, all without a single line of narration.

Environmental storytelling is fully multi-sensory. Every environment has a deliberate acoustic identity, so a player who cannot see the space still learns its history through sound, and haptic and descriptive layers carry the same information for a player who cannot hear it. The environment is read, not merely seen.

- Acoustic evidence, such as the way a space rings, hums, drips, or falls silent, tells the player what a place is and what happened there
- Physical evidence that can be examined by touch, such as wear, damage, temperature, and arrangement, records use and history
- Descriptive narration through Faience and the interface makes the same evidence available to players who prefer or require it
- The environment answers questions the player did not know to ask, which invites the next question

## Hidden journals

Personal writing left behind by the people of the world is one of the richest forms of discovery. A hidden journal is a voice, and finding one means meeting a person the player may never otherwise encounter. Journals humanise history and turn statistics into individuals.

Hidden journals are presented in whatever form suits the finder. Text can be read visually, read aloud, rendered in braille, or delivered as recorded speech, and the emotional tone of a journal is carried in its acoustic reading as much as in its words. A journal is never a wall of text that only sighted readers can use.

- Journals reveal the interior life behind an event the player has seen from the outside
- Journals are discovered through exploration, repair, and conversation rather than handed out on a schedule
- Journals feed the Knowledge Archive so that a personal account becomes part of the community's record
- Journals can change how the player understands a place, a person, or a decision already made

## Optional conversations

Some of the most important discoveries are spoken by people who will only share them with a player who takes the time to talk. Optional conversations are never filler; they are where expertise, memory, and relationship live. The player is never the smartest person in the room, and conversation is how the room's knowledge becomes available.

Optional conversations respect every communication style. A conversation may unfold through speech, sign language, augmentative and alternative communication, or written exchange, and the discovery it carries is identical regardless of the method used. Interruptions, pauses, and silence within a conversation are themselves information.

- Conversations reveal knowledge that exists only from a particular person's perspective
- Conversations deepen relationships, and deeper relationships open further conversations
- Conversations are optional in most cases, yet they consistently unlock strategic and emotional understanding
- Conversations record what was learned so that it can change later dialogue and choices

## Scientific discoveries

Scientific discovery is the disciplined side of curiosity, where observation becomes hypothesis and hypothesis becomes understanding. In Resonance, science is a way of thinking rather than a menu of upgrades, and it treats the unknown as something to understand rather than to conquer.

Scientific discovery is gradual and evidence-led. A first observation raises a question, further study refines it, and the conclusion arrives only when the evidence supports it. This mirrors the universe's rule that understanding is corrected by new evidence rather than by rewriting the past.

- Scientific discovery begins with observation and proceeds through evidence rather than instant revelation
- Scientific discovery changes what the player can do in research, engineering, and medicine
- Scientific discovery is available through instruments that report acoustically, haptically, and descriptively, not only visually
- Scientific discovery raises new questions as it settles old ones, keeping curiosity ahead of certainty

## Historical discoveries

History in Resonance is recovered rather than recited. A historical discovery reconstructs what happened from the evidence that survives, and it is always framed as current understanding open to revision. The world had a history before the player arrived, and every persistent world keeps its own record of what its community has built and lived.

Historical discovery honours the difference between objective history and historical knowledge. The player pieces together an account from fragments, and later evidence can deepen or correct that account without dishonouring the earlier, honest attempt to understand it.

- Historical discovery reconstructs events from surviving evidence rather than delivering a finished narrative
- Historical discovery is provisional, and new evidence corrects it through further discovery rather than retcon
- Historical discovery enters the Knowledge Archive as part of a community's living record
- Historical discovery changes how the player interprets the places and people connected to that history

## Secret rooms

A secret room is a reward for curiosity about space itself. Finding one means the player noticed something the world did not announce, whether an unusual echo, a draught, a seam in a wall, or a corridor that does not match the map. Secret rooms make attention tangible.

Secret rooms are discoverable without sight. Their presence is carried acoustically through how sound behaves near a concealed space, and through haptic and descriptive cues, so that a blind player has the same genuine chance to find them as a sighted player. A secret that could only be seen would violate the accessibility-first foundation of the franchise.

- Secret rooms reward spatial curiosity and careful listening to how a space behaves
- Secret rooms are found through acoustic, haptic, and descriptive cues rather than sight alone
- Secret rooms contain discoveries that matter, such as journals, history, tools, or community stories, never empty spectacle
- Secret rooms deepen the player's sense that the world holds more than it shows

## Maintenance secrets

Maintenance is a way of seeing the ship and the home from underneath, and it exposes truths that no other perspective reaches. Behind panels and inside conduits lie the honest record of how a place actually works, who kept it working, and what went wrong. Maintenance secrets are the world's memory of its own upkeep.

Maintenance discovery is inseparable from acoustic identity. A failing system sounds wrong before it looks wrong, and a maintenance worker learns to read a space by ear and by hand. The discoveries found this way change repair, safety, and the player's understanding of the community that depends on the work.

- Maintenance secrets reveal how a place truly functions and who has cared for it
- Maintenance secrets are read through sound and touch as readily as through sight
- Maintenance secrets change engineering options, safety, and the reliability of the home
- Maintenance secrets connect the player to the often unseen labour that sustains community life

## Engineering secrets

Engineering discovery concerns the deeper design of systems, including the reasons behind how something was built and the possibilities that design still holds. Where maintenance reveals the current state of a system, engineering reveals its intent and its potential. Understanding a system's design unlocks new ways to use, adapt, and improve it.

Engineering secrets are frequently tied to the Shawabti legacy, and where they touch that legacy the game reveals mechanism and capability progressively through play. It never pre-empts the mysteries the campaigns are built to explore.

- Engineering secrets reveal the intent and untapped potential behind a system's design
- Engineering secrets unlock new capabilities in construction, adaptation, and problem solving
- Engineering secrets that touch the Shawabti legacy are revealed gradually rather than explained at once
- Engineering secrets raise new questions about what a system could yet become

## Medical discoveries

Medical discovery is understanding a body, a condition, a treatment, or a history of care. In Resonance, medicine accommodates every kind of body as an ordinary expectation, and medical discovery expands the community's ability to care for its people rather than merely to repair them.

Medical discovery is handled with dignity. It never treats disability as a puzzle to be solved or a flaw to be erased, and it aligns with the canon that disability shapes characters without defining them. What the player learns changes the care the community can offer and the understanding the player carries.

- Medical discovery expands the community's ability to care for every kind of body
- Medical discovery is presented respectfully and never frames disability as a defect to correct
- Medical discovery is accessible through acoustic, haptic, and descriptive reporting, not sight alone
- Medical discovery changes treatment options, dialogue with patients, and player understanding

## Research discoveries

Research discovery is the sustained, deliberate pursuit of a question over time, often carried out alongside Faience as research partner and historian. Research turns scattered observations into a coherent understanding, and it belongs to the player through the persistent Knowledge Archive.

Research is optional but consequential. A player who ignores research can still complete the game while facing more combat, more uncertainty, and fewer strategic options, which keeps curiosity meaningful without ever gating the game behind it.

- Research discovery synthesises many observations into durable understanding
- Research discovery is optional yet consistently expands the player's strategic choices
- Research discovery is stored in the player's persistent archive and belongs to the player
- Research discovery reliably produces new questions worth pursuing

## Accessibility discoveries

Accessibility itself is a subject of discovery, and this is central to Resonance rather than incidental. Players discover accessible techniques, tools, and design principles, and they come to understand that accessibility is standard equipment rather than special accommodation. Some of the deepest revelations of Operation One concern accessibility as a foundation.

Accessibility discovery is never framed as a workaround or a cheat. Learning an accessible method is presented as learning a better, more universal way to do something, in keeping with the franchise principle that no information ever depends on a single sense.

- Accessibility discovery teaches techniques and tools that are treated as ordinary equipment
- Accessibility discovery reframes accessible design as universal design rather than accommodation
- Accessibility discovery changes how the player and the community navigate, communicate, and build
- Accessibility discovery connects to a foundational truth about the Shawabti that is understood only gradually through play

## Community discoveries

Community discovery is learning the people, relationships, routines, and history of the living world around the player. Because community is progression, understanding the community is a genuine form of advancement. The world continues when the player is away, so returning often reveals what has changed and grown.

Community discovery is heard before it is seen. The soundscape grows as the community grows, and a player often learns that something has changed by ear before any visual confirmation, consistent with the rule that sound is progression.

- Community discovery reveals people, relationships, routines, and the shared history of a place
- Community discovery is often perceived acoustically before it is confirmed visually
- Community discovery deepens belonging and opens new conversations and collaborations
- Community discovery reflects a world that keeps living whether or not the player is present

## Player-created discoveries

Players make discoveries that were not individually authored, arising instead from the interaction of systems, the persistence of the world, and the ways players and communities behave. A world accumulates a history of its own, and players contribute to that history rather than owning it, so discovery emerges from lived play as well as from designed content.

Player-created discoveries are honoured by the persistent world. What a community builds, records, and leaves behind becomes discoverable by others, and returning players receive meaningful summaries of what happened while they were away, so that emergent history is surfaced rather than lost.

- Player-created discoveries emerge from systems, persistence, and community behaviour rather than from a single authored moment
- Player-created discoveries become part of a world's own accumulating history
- Player-created discoveries are surfaced through summaries, records, and the shared Knowledge Archive
- Player-created discoveries let one community's understanding become discoverable by another

## Knowledge Archive discoveries

The Knowledge Archive is where discovery is recorded, connected, and made consequential, and it is stored as part of the player's persistent memory. A discovery that enters the archive is cross-referenced with what the player already knows, and new entries can cast earlier ones in a new light. The Knowledge Archive Bible governs how learning works in full, and this document defers to it.

Archive discovery is the act of noticing connections across everything gathered. Two fragments that meant little apart can, once archived together, answer a question or open a larger one. Every archive entry must eventually change something, or it is not built.

- Knowledge Archive discovery connects separate findings into new understanding
- Knowledge Archive discovery is persistent and belongs to the player across the campaign
- Knowledge Archive discovery must change dialogue, exploration, or capability rather than sit as lore
- Knowledge Archive discovery is corrected by further discovery rather than by rewriting earlier honest entries

## Resonance Logs

Resonance Logs are the means by which the Shawabti preserved not only facts but the relationships, values, and ways of thinking behind them, and the Living Archive communicates them through Resonance. Encountering a Resonance Log is a profound form of discovery, and the player's ability to interpret one grows as the campaign teaches them how.

Resonance Logs touch the deliberate mysteries of the franchise, so the game describes how a log is gradually interpreted and where its meaning is recorded, without pre-empting conclusions the story has not yet reached. What a Resonance Log ultimately reveals about the Shawabti and about Faience unfolds progressively through play.

- Resonance Logs preserve meaning, relationship, and ways of thinking rather than isolated facts
- Resonance Logs are interpreted gradually as the player learns the skill of reading them
- Resonance Logs are experienced acoustically and multi-sensorially, in keeping with the Audio Bible
- Resonance Logs advance the central mysteries without resolving them ahead of the story

## Progressive mystery solving

Progressive mystery solving is the mechanism by which the largest questions of Resonance are approached over time. The franchise contains deliberate mysteries, above all those surrounding the Shawabti and Faience, and this document describes only how revelation happens, never what the answers are. The mysteries are preserved, and the player's understanding of them deepens step by step.

The mechanism is cumulative and evidence-led. Fragments gathered across places, people, logs, and campaigns accumulate until a fuller picture forms, and understanding is corrected through further discovery rather than through retcon. A revelation always opens a larger question, so certainty never fully closes and curiosity always has somewhere to go.

- Progressive mystery solving reveals understanding gradually through accumulated evidence, never all at once
- Progressive mystery solving preserves the deliberate mysteries and never states their answers here
- Progressive mystery solving records each step where the campaign reveals it rather than pre-announcing conclusions
- Progressive mystery solving ends each stage by opening a larger question than it answered

## How discovery changes gameplay

Discovery is progression, so learning something changes what the player is able to do. A discovery may open a new route, a new repair, a new treatment, a new negotiation, or a new way to navigate, and it does so because understanding, not an arbitrary unlock, has changed. The player becomes more capable for the same reason the story becomes more complex.

Every meaningful discovery must change at least one gameplay dimension, and if it would change nothing it is not built. This keeps the world responsive to curiosity and keeps knowledge from decaying into collectible lore.

- Discovery opens new actions in exploration, engineering, medicine, research, and diplomacy
- Discovery reflects genuine understanding rather than an arbitrary gate lifted
- Discovery must change at least one gameplay dimension to justify its existence
- Discovery keeps player capability and story complexity growing together

## How discovery changes dialogue

What the player knows changes how people speak to them. A crew member talks differently to a player who has understood their work, read their journal, or grasped the history they carry, and conversations that were closed can open once the player brings the right understanding. Dialogue is one of the clearest places where knowledge must change something.

Discovery also changes the player's own contributions to conversation. New understanding gives the player new questions to ask and new things to say, and it lets Faience surface relevant knowledge at the moments it matters. Dialogue reflects a world that remembers what the player has learned.

- Discovery unlocks new lines, topics, and questions the player can raise
- Discovery changes how characters respond, reflecting what the player now understands
- Discovery lets Faience surface archived knowledge at the moment it becomes relevant
- Discovery makes conversation a record of understanding rather than a fixed script

## How discovery changes future campaigns

Discovery persists beyond the moment and beyond the chapter. Because knowledge belongs to the player and every world keeps its own history, what is discovered carries forward, shaping later chapters, later operations, and the understanding a player brings to a new perspective. Operation One happens once, and replaying it through another profession reveals further pieces of the same fixed history.

Across campaigns, understanding accumulates rather than resets. Later campaigns build on what earlier discovery established, correcting and deepening it through new evidence rather than rewriting it, so the franchise grows as a single, consistent, expanding body of understanding.

- Discovery persists across chapters, operations, and perspectives rather than resetting
- Discovery carried into a new profession reveals further pieces of the same fixed history
- Discovery in later campaigns builds on earlier understanding rather than overwriting it
- Discovery accumulates into a single, consistent, expanding franchise understanding
