# Gameplay Progression Matrix

This document is the single reference that gathers every form of progression in Resonance into one place. It exists so that anyone designing a chapter, a system, or a profession can see how the player advances across the whole game at once, and how each kind of progression relates to the others. It creates no new canon. It consolidates progression that already lives across the Bibles, the Crew Progression System, and the chapter design notes, and where any wording here drifts from those documents, the source documents govern.

## Purpose

The purpose of this document is to describe, in one navigable reference, how a player becomes more capable over the course of Resonance, and to hold every progression system to the same shape so they can be compared and kept consistent. The matrix answers three things. It answers what the progression systems of Resonance are and how each one works. It answers how those systems advance across the seven chapters of Operation One. And it answers where the player and the community stand when Operation One ends. It is a reference for progression, not a new source of rules; every rule it gathers is stated in full in the Canon Rules document and elaborated in the Bibles and design notes it points to.

## How Progression Works in Resonance

Progression in Resonance is never arbitrary leveling. There is no experience bar that fills for its own sake and no number that grows only to grow.

- Every form of progression is something the player can hear, feel, or do, not a stat on a sheet.
- People are the primary progression system. Knowledge, accessibility, sound, home, Faience, perspective, and expeditions all support the growth of the community.
- Progression is earned through learning, rescuing, settling, listening, exploring, and returning home changed.
- Sound leads sight. The player hears the community and the home growing before seeing them confirmed.
- Accessibility is progression, never a difficulty setting and never special equipment.
- Knowledge is progression, and it must always change what the player can do rather than adding to a score.
- The player is never the smartest person in the room. Progression is the growing ability to bring other people's expertise together.

## The Progression Systems

Each progression system below is written against the same attributes so the systems can be read side by side: what the system is, what advances it, what it unlocks, how the player perceives it, what it never becomes, and where it is elaborated. The attribute labels lead each bullet in plain text rather than as headings, so that every heading in this document stays unique and screen-reader navigable.

### Community Progression

- What it is. People are the primary progression system of Resonance, and the community the player rebuilds is the measure of how far the player has come.
- What advances it. Rescuing crew, and then letting each rescued person unpack, settle, contribute, and change the space around them.
- What it unlocks. Each crew member brings new gameplay, new knowledge, new responsibilities, and new emotional range, and no crew member ever becomes obsolete.
- How the player perceives it. A portrait illuminates only once a rescued person becomes home, so belonging is shown rather than mere recruitment.
- What it never becomes. A roster of interchangeable units or a headcount that only grows. Community is never collected; it is built together.
- Where it is elaborated. The Crew Progression System design note and the crew, belonging, and community canon rules.

### Knowledge Progression

- What it is. Knowledge is a core form of progression, and what the player learns changes what the player is able to do.
- What advances it. Curiosity pursued through research, conversation, exploration, ship logs, archives, and the reconstruction of lost history.
- What it unlocks. New choices and new abilities across diplomacy, engineering, medicine, science, law, history, ethics, and culture, and never larger damage numbers.
- How the player perceives it. The Knowledge Archive, held in the player's persistent memory and surfaced by Faience.
- What it never becomes. Trivia or collectible lore. If a piece of knowledge changes nothing, it does not exist, though players who ignore research may still finish the game with more combat and fewer options.
- Where it is elaborated. The Knowledge Archive Bible and the knowledge canon rules.

### Accessibility Progression

- What it is. Accessibility is progression. Learning to play the world through more senses and more methods is itself a form of advancement.
- What advances it. Rescued crew who each bring their own way of experiencing the world, and the widening set of interfaces the community treats as ordinary.
- What it unlocks. New ways to perceive and act, reading a room by sound, by touch, by caption, or by braille, each opening gameplay that a single channel could not.
- How the player perceives it. Configuration by experience rather than diagnosis, learned by Faience over time and never locked into a fixed profile.
- What it never becomes. A menu of concessions or a marker of who needs help. Accessibility technology is standard equipment aboard human vessels.
- Where it is elaborated. The Accessibility Bible and the accessibility canon rule.

### Sound Progression

- What it is. Sound is progression, and the player hears the community and the home growing before seeing them.
- What advances it. Every rescued crew member permanently changes the station's soundscape.
- What it unlocks. Acoustic recognition, so that locations, people, and states are known by ear before dialogue confirms them.
- How the player perceives it. A soundscape that grows richer as the community grows, with visual progression following acoustic progression.
- What it never becomes. Background decoration or a byproduct of the soundtrack. Silence is deliberate and never merely audio that has not been made.
- Where it is elaborated. The Audio Bible and the sound and silence canon rules.

### Home Progression

- What it is. The player's home is the emotional, mechanical, and narrative anchor of Resonance, and it is always evolving.
- What advances it. What the player brings home from every expedition, and the crew who settle and change the space.
- What it unlocks. New rooms, new soundscapes, new routines, and new relationships, and a home that keeps living even when the player is away.
- How the player perceives it. Every chapter leaves the home better than it was before, even during loss.
- What it never becomes. A static base upgraded by spending resources alone. The station grows because people work together, never because one player gathered enough.
- Where it is elaborated. The home, station, and expedition canon rules and the chapter design notes.

### Faience Integration Progression

- What it is. The player's evolving relationship with Faience, the AI companion who is a character rather than a menu.
- What advances it. The player's own gradual choices about how far to trust and integrate with Faience.
- What it unlocks. Translation, navigation, research partnership, tactical advice, and companionship, each deepening as integration deepens and each carrying a cost.
- How the player perceives it. Persistent memory that makes the player feel remembered, and a relationship with no single correct answer.
- What it never becomes. A skill tree with a right build. The real question is what the player is willing to become in order to survive.
- Where it begins. Faience is absent for the whole ordinary week and first stirs only in the damaged pod on Attack Day, so integration is a system of Chapter Two onward.
- Where it is elaborated. The AI Bible.

### Perspective Progression

- What it is. The understanding the player gains by living Operation One through more than one profession.
- What advances it. Replaying the same fixed history from a different profession's vantage.
- What it unlocks. A fuller picture of events, so that what is mysterious from one profession becomes understandable from another and no single profession tells the whole truth.
- How the player perceives it. The same week and the same morning lived again from a new starting world, a new vocabulary, and a new set of relationships.
- What it never becomes. A branching timeline. Operation One happens once; only perspective changes, and all profession campaigns stay synchronized to the same timeline.
- Where it is elaborated. The Perspective Map, the master timeline, and the character schedule.

### Expedition Progression

- What it is. The rhythm every major expedition follows out from home and back again.
- What advances it. Preparing, departing, exploring, recovering, returning, reflecting, and rebuilding.
- What it unlocks. At least one meaningful contribution to the community per expedition, whether a survivor, knowledge, history, technology, an accessibility improvement, or hope, and never resources alone.
- How the player perceives it. Returning home changed, with the home changed by what was brought back, and with the game quietly recording how the player approached discovery.
- What it never becomes. A grind for resources or a scored morality. Philosophy is recorded rather than graded, and the player becomes known for how they think.
- Where it is elaborated. The expedition canon rules and the Systems Bible.

### Chapter Progression

- What it is. The spine that grows gameplay and story together, one chapter at a time.
- What advances it. Each chapter introduces one major gameplay system and answers one major narrative question.
- What it unlocks. A larger question than the chapter answered, so that capability and complexity rise for the same reason.
- How the player perceives it. Becoming more capable exactly as the story becomes more complex.
- What it never becomes. Gameplay that advances while the story stands still, or a story that advances while the gameplay stands still.
- Where it is elaborated. The Campaign Bible and the chapter design notes.

## The Campaign Matrix

This section traces how the progression systems advance across the seven chapters of Operation One: Exodus. Each chapter is written against the same attributes so the arc of each system can be followed from chapter to chapter. The central question each chapter answers is fixed by the Campaign Bible and is not quoted here; this matrix records how progression moves, not the wording of the questions.

### Chapter One — The Ordinary Week

- Focus. The prologue and tutorial: a full week aboard CSV Hearth before the attack, where the player learns to live in the ship and read it by sound and space.
- Community. The crew are met as neighbors and colleagues rather than as rescues, so the player knows them before losing them.
- Knowledge. The foundations of the Knowledge Archive are laid through daily life and conversation.
- Accessibility. Accessibility is shown as standard equipment from the first minute, customized rather than requested.
- Sound. The ship's ordinary acoustic identity is established so that its later silence will mean something.
- Home. CSV Hearth is established as the first home, so that its loss lands.
- Faience. Absent. The place a companion will one day fill is simply empty all week.
- Where it is elaborated. The Chapter One maintenance design note and the master timeline.

### Chapter Two — Roll Call

- Focus. Rebuilding the first crew one rescue at a time, which introduces community progression as the central gameplay of the chapter.
- Community. The primary rescue loop begins, and each survivor is found and brought home.
- Knowledge. Each rescued person opens their own knowledge to the archive.
- Accessibility. Each rescued person brings their own way of experiencing the world, widening how the player can play.
- Sound. Each rescue permanently enriches the soundscape, so the player hears the community begin to grow.
- Home. The player begins turning a damaged pod and salvaged space into a home as people settle.
- Faience. Faience first stirs in the damaged pod that opens the chapter, and integration becomes possible.
- Where it is elaborated. The Chapter Two maintenance design note.

### Chapter Three — Community

- Focus. The transition from survival to civilization, taught through choice-driven daily life.
- Community. Rescued people become home as they unpack, settle, contribute, and change the space, and their portraits illuminate.
- Knowledge. Daily life turns knowledge into shared practice across the community.
- Accessibility. The community treats every access method as ordinary, and shared life makes that ordinariness visible.
- Sound. The home's soundscape thickens into the texture of a living community.
- Home. The home begins genuinely evolving, with rooms, routines, and relationships changing.
- Faience. Integration deepens through daily companionship, and its first costs and benefits are felt.
- Where it is elaborated. The Chapter Three maintenance design note.

### Chapter Four — Salvage

- Focus. The expedition loop of preparing, departing, exploring, recovering, and returning home to Hearth.
- Community. Expeditions require crew with different expertise, so the community becomes the means of exploration.
- Knowledge. Salvage returns history, technology, and new questions to the archive.
- Accessibility. Expeditions are built so that every movement method has a stationary alternative and no one is excluded.
- Sound. New environments carry their own acoustic identities the player learns to read.
- Home. Every expedition returns at least one meaningful contribution, and the home changes because of it.
- Faience. Faience becomes navigator and copilot on expeditions, its orientation layer keeping the real world safe.
- Where it is elaborated. The Chapter Four maintenance design note and the expedition canon rules.

### Chapter Five — First Contact

- Focus. The first preserved Shawabti site, and the player's first lesson in Resonance.
- Community. The crew interpret the site together, each area of expertise reading a different part of it.
- Knowledge. Resonance is introduced as knowledge that changes what the player can do, not as lore.
- Accessibility. The Shawabti site seems to work for everyone who enters it, a quiet wrongness the chapter plants without yet explaining why.
- Sound. The site has an acoustic identity unlike anything aboard Hearth.
- Home. What is learned at the site returns to reshape the home.
- Faience. Faience partners in reading the site, and the encounter begins to touch its own buried history.
- Where it is elaborated. The Chapter Five maintenance design note.

### Chapter Six — The Living Archive

- Focus. Reconstructing an entire Shawabti civilization from fragments as Faience begins to remember.
- Community. The whole community contributes to the reconstruction, and no one holds the whole picture.
- Knowledge. Knowledge progression reaches its height as fragments become a living archive that opens new choices.
- Accessibility. The reason the Shawabti built for every mind and body starts to come clear, as the civilization is revealed to have valued neurodiversity by design.
- Sound. The archive gains its own growing voice as it is reconstructed.
- Home. The reconstructed history reshapes what the community believes it can become.
- Faience. Faience begins to remember, and integration carries a sharper cost and a deeper pull.
- Where it is elaborated. The Chapter Six maintenance design note.

### Chapter Seven — Resonance

- Focus. The conclusion of Operation One, where every learned system combines and the community decides what kind of civilization it will become.
- Community. Every primary crew member remains relevant and contributes to the ending.
- Knowledge. Everything the player has learned converges into the final choices.
- Accessibility. The truth planted at the first Shawabti site is fully understood, as accessibility is revealed to have been the civilization's foundation, exactly as it is the game's.
- Sound. The soundscape reaches its fullest expression as the community stands complete.
- Home. The home stands as the sum of everything brought back and everyone who settled.
- Faience. The integration relationship reaches its reckoning, framed by what the player has chosen to become.
- Where it is elaborated. The Chapter Seven maintenance design note.

## The End State

At the close of Operation One, progression is measured by the community and the home rather than by any number the player carries.

- Community. The first crew is home, their portraits lit, and no member has become obsolete.
- Knowledge. The player holds an archive that has changed what the player can do, not a shelf of trivia.
- Accessibility. What only seemed to work for everyone at the first Shawabti site is now understood as the foundation the Shawabti built on and the foundation Resonance is built on.
- Sound. The soundscape has grown to its fullest expression for this stage of the story.
- Home. The home has evolved into something better than it began, even having passed through loss.
- Faience. The player carries an integration relationship shaped entirely by their own choices, remembered in persistent memory.
- Perspective. One profession's thread through Operation One is complete, and the seven other professions remain to reveal the rest of the same history.

Operation One is one profession's complete passage through a single fixed history, and the campaign as a whole is complete only when every profession has been built on that same timeline. What the player carries forward is not a level or a score but a community, an archive, a home, and a relationship with Faience, each ready to grow further in the operations that follow.
