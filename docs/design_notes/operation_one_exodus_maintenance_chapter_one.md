# Operation One: Exodus — Chapter One: A Week Aboard Hearth — Maintenance Perspective

## Purpose

Chapter One defines the first major playable prologue for Resonance: Fractured Frequency.

The player experiences approximately one week aboard CSV Hearth before the attack.

This chapter is not a tutorial hallway.

It is the player's first home.

The goal is for the player to know the ship, its people, its sounds, its routines, and its emotional life before the attack begins.

Faience is not active yet.

The player begins with routine, work, people, sound, and home.

## Core Design Philosophy

Chapter One teaches through ordinary life.

Do not use traditional pop-up tutorials unless absolutely necessary.

The player should feel like they are learning their job, not learning a game.

Sound is the primary renderer of the world.

Every sound communicates information.

Accessibility technology is ordinary aboard Hearth.

Nobody treats access technology as unusual.

The player should lose a home, not just a level.

## Ship Identity

The ship is:

CSV Hearth

Registry:

CSV-0101-H

CSV means Colony Settlement Vessel.

0101 intentionally references binary while remaining an original registry system.

H designates the Hearth-class colony ark.

CSV Hearth is a civilian colony ark.

It is not a warship.

It should evoke the emotional feeling of the USS Enterprise NCC-1701-D: safe, welcoming, lived-in, familiar, and community-centred, without copying Star Trek.

Hearth carries a full society, not just passengers.

It contains:

Housing

Schools

Medical facilities

Research labs

Engineering

Maintenance infrastructure

Hydroponics and agriculture

Manufacturing

Recreation spaces

Places of worship

Governance

Families

The player should learn Hearth by sound.

Each deck has its own acoustic identity.

## Primary Crew Canon

Chapter One introduces one important NPC connected to each playable profession.

These NPCs later become the player's first rescued crew.

When Hearth is destroyed, each primary NPC escapes in a separate escape pod or survival craft.

Chapter Two is built around locating, identifying, boarding, and rescuing every one of them.

The player may not progress beyond Chapter Two until every primary NPC has been found and rescued.

Each rescue teaches one major gameplay system.

Rescue is progression.

People are progression.

Technology alone is never enough.

## Crew Design Principle

Every primary crew member represents a different adaptation to life.

Disability, neurodivergence, chronic illness, and access technology are ordinary parts of society aboard CSV Hearth.

Technology supports people.

Technology does not erase identity.

No crew member exists only to represent a diagnosis.

Every crew member is first a person.

The Shawabti later reveal they valued adaptive diversity because different minds and bodies consistently solved different deep-space problems.

Senior Medical Officer.

Deaf.

Crew may sign, speak, or type.

The responsibility for communication is shared rather than placed entirely on Samira.

Medical systems.

Maintenance Supervisor.

Chief Engineering Systems Specialist.

Condition:

Blind.

Presentation:

Mara wears an engineering helmet with a specialised navigation faceplate.

The faceplate is human-built, not Shawabti technology.

### Dr. Soren Qadir

Role:

Senior Research Scientist.

Condition:

Wheelchair user.

Presentation:

Soren's powered chair uses compact repulsor discs while charged.

The chair hovers several centimetres above the deck during normal operation.

If battery power becomes critically low, the repulsor system safely disengages and the chair converts to manual wheel mode.

The player may choose to push the chair, allow Soren to self-propel, recharge the chair, or find another transportation solution.

The chair is ordinary ship equipment rather than spectacle.

Gameplay taught later:

Research.

Scientific reasoning.

Energy management.

Shawabti investigation.

### Mira Ives

Role:

Civilian child.

Condition:

Autistic and ADHD with complex communication disability.

Presentation:

Mira communicates primarily through an AAC system controlled by a visible neural interface.

The chip/interface should be visible behind one ear and across part of the scalp.

It is ordinary technology aboard Hearth.

Adults wait for Mira's responses without impatience.

Mira should quietly say something about the game: wonder, communication, patience, and why people matter.

Different communication styles.

Hope.

## Chapter Structure

Chapter One is divided into short named parts, similar to sections in a Stephen King novel.

Each part should feel like a slice of life aboard Hearth.

The chapter covers about one week using selected playable scenes, not seven repetitive full days.

## Part One: The Ship Wakes

Time:

Day One, 06:25 to 06:45 Ship Standard Time.

Purpose:

Introduce waking aboard Hearth.

The player wakes to ship sounds before words.

Soundscape:

Night ventilation shifting into day-cycle airflow.

Distant engines.

Low structural hum.

Doors opening down the corridor.

Galley equipment starting.

Coffee brewing.

Quiet crew movement.

Someone laughing softly.

Mira running and being gently corrected.

A maintenance drone rolling past.

At exactly 06:30 Ship Standard Time, a gentle two-tone morning claxon sounds.

Captain Elian of the Vale System gives the morning announcement in the warm morning-announcement voice:

"Good morning, everyone.

This is Captain Elian of the Vale System.

It is now 0630 Ship Standard Time.

Morning shift begins in thirty minutes.

Whether you're heading to Engineering, Medical, Security, Research, Flight Operations, Maintenance, or just starting another day with your family, thank you for everything you do to keep this ship alive.

If today is your day off, enjoy it.

If today is your first day aboard, welcome home.

For those coming off night shift, thank you for keeping us moving while the rest of us slept.

Have a good morning, everyone."

Player actions:

Wake.

Orient in quarters.

Locate the door by sound.

Leave quarters.

Enter corridor.

Mechanics taught:

Basic listening.

Room acoustics.

Door interaction.

Corridor orientation.

Movement by sound.

End condition:

Player exits crew quarters and begins moving toward maintenance briefing.

## Part Two: The Corridor Knows Your Name

Time:

Day One morning.

Purpose:

Introduce Hearth as inhabited.

The ship should feel like a city, not a level.

Soundscape:

Crew walking.

Distant conversation.

Family breakfast preparation.

Utility carts.

Soft ship announcements.

People greeting each other by name.

Maintenance drone traffic.

NPC moments:

A crew member says good morning.

Mira asks why maintenance people always carry tools, using AAC.

Someone jokes Engineering broke something again.

A maintenance drone nearly bumps into the player and chirps an apology.

Mechanics taught:

Following voices.

Avoiding moving objects by sound.

Recognising corridor branches.

Using environmental audio to stay oriented.

End condition:

Player reaches maintenance briefing.

## Part Three: The People Who Keep the Ship Alive

Time:

Day One morning briefing.

Purpose:

Introduce Maintenance culture and Rowan Ives.

Maintenance are the people who know Hearth by ear.

They hear pumps before alarms do.

They know when a corridor sounds wrong.

They do not simply repair the ship.

They know the ship like a body.

Rowan teaches through ordinary work, not speeches.

Briefing content:

Deck Twelve has intermittent pressure warnings.

Life support in Section C is acting up again.

Inspection drones are being overridden somewhere.

Pressure Junction 14 needs a routine check later in the week.

Research has requested restricted maintenance access but has not explained why.

Engineering is annoyed about unexplained power draws.

Mechanics taught:

Listening to objective information.

Checking task list.

Understanding that Maintenance hears the ship differently.

End condition:

Player receives shift tasks and heads toward the public crew space.

## Part Four: Coffee Before Systems

Time:

Day One morning.

Purpose:

Introduce the social heart of Hearth.

Coffee or breakfast is mandatory as a location, not necessarily as an item.

The player must pass through the public crew space so the ship becomes emotionally real.

Soundscape:

Coffee machine.

Trays.

Utensils.

Soft crowd noise.

Children.

Families.

Officers.

Medical staff.

Security personnel.

Research staff.

Maintenance crew.

Civilians.

NPC introduction opportunities:

Doctor Samira Kade uses her sleeve projector while speaking with crew.

Talia Rusk complains about new restricted zones.

Mara Chen identifies a minor systems problem by sound before others notice it.

Jonah Briggs redirects traffic and keeps people moving.

Dr. Soren Qadir's repulsor chair hum is heard before he speaks about restricted research access.

Mira Ives uses AAC to ask a question or respond to an adult.

Mechanics taught:

Sound in crowded spaces.

Finding a target sound.

Optional conversation.

Social navigation.

Emotional purpose:

The player should hear the people they may later try to save.

End condition:

Player leaves crew space and begins first maintenance route.

## Part Five: Pressure Junction 14

Time:

Day Two.

Purpose:

First audio maintenance puzzle.

This is the first time the player learns that sound communicates system state.

Setup:

The player inspects Pressure Junction 14 as routine maintenance.

It is not an emergency.

It is normal work.

Puzzle:

Three sound sources are present.

One pipe hums steadily.

One pipe rattles.

One pipe whistles.

The whistling pressure regulator is wrong.

The player must identify and interact with the whistling regulator.

Teaching pattern:

Normal sound.

Warning sound.

Critical sound.

The whistle begins faintly.

As the player approaches, it becomes clearer.

If ignored too long, it develops into a stronger harmonic vibration.

Success:

The whistle stops.

The pipe returns to smooth rhythmic airflow.

Rowan confirms the repair.

Design rule:

Do not use visual flashing indicators as the primary clue.

The sound is the clue.

End condition:

Pressure Junction 14 stabilises.

Time:

Locations:

Service corridors.

Crawlspaces.

Maintenance ladders.

Utility junctions.

Soundscape:

Closer machinery.

Tighter echoes.

Vent fans.

Pipe resonance.

Electrical relays.

Distant crew muffled through walls.

Player actions:

Move through a service route.

Identify a stuck pressure door.

Bypass a minor system fault.

Emerge somewhere unexpected.

Mechanics taught:

Spatial memory.

Tight-space navigation.

Alternate route logic.

Maintenance-only access.

Recognising ship layers by sound.

Emotional purpose:

The player should feel they know Hearth intimately.

Not as a map.

As a body.

End condition:

Player completes hidden-route task and returns to normal crew areas.

## Part Seven: Little Emergencies

Time:

Day Five.

Purpose:

Introduce urgency without combat.

Incident:

Mira Ives gets into a restricted maintenance passage and becomes frightened when a pressure door locks behind her.

Player actions:

Follow Mira's voice and AAC output cues.

Scan the pressure door.

Identify the lock fault.

Use maintenance access.

Calm Mira.

Open the door or route around it.

Mechanics taught:

Following a distressed voice.

Following AAC, tactile, or electronic communication cues.

Prioritising sound under stress.

Repair under light pressure.

NPC reassurance.

Non-combat rescue.

Emotional purpose:

Maintenance protects people, not just machines.

End condition:

Mira is safe.

Rowan thanks the player.

Mira remembers the player.

## Part Eight: The Wrong Sound

Time:

Day Six.

Purpose:

Foreshadow the attack and the artefact.

The player notices things that do not fit.

Soundscape changes:

Engine pitch shifts slightly.

Research deck has unusual security.

Some doors are quieter than they should be.

Restricted areas have new patrol sounds.

A system vibration appears briefly and disappears.

A comm channel cuts out mid-sentence.

Overheard line:

"Tell Research if that thing pulls power from life support again, Maintenance is going to start asking questions."

Player choice:

Report anomaly.

Or ignore anomaly.

Canon rule:

This choice affects Chapter Two.

If the player reports the anomaly:

Maintenance begins Chapter Two with better situational awareness.

Additional emergency bulkheads remain operational.

More environmental information is available.

Some escape pods are located more quickly.

Certain NPC rescue missions become less dangerous.

If the player ignores the anomaly:

The attack follows a different damage pattern.

Additional systems fail unexpectedly.

Escape pod telemetry is more fragmented.

Search operations become significantly more difficult.

Alternative routes must be discovered.

Neither path is a good ending or bad ending.

Choices create different histories.

Design rule:

Do not explain the artefact yet.

Make the player curious.

End condition:

Player logs or ignores the unexplained system anomaly.

## Part Nine: A Normal Morning

Time:

Day Seven, 06:25 to 06:35 Ship Standard Time.

Purpose:

Mirror Part One.

The familiar morning rhythm returns.

The player expects safety.

Soundscape:

Ventilation.

Engines.

Doors.

Coffee.

Crew.

Children.

Maintenance drone.

Routine claxon.

At 06:30 the morning claxon sounds.

Captain Elian of the Vale System begins in the warm morning-announcement voice:

"Good morning, everyone.

This is Captain Elian of the Vale System.

It is now 0630 Ship Standard Time—"

Then something changes.

A pause.

A sound under the ship.

A deep impossible vibration.

Emergency traffic bleeds under the announcement.

Captain Naomi of the Vale System takes command.

The voice shifts into the emergency command cue:

Lower.

Firmer.

Short precise sentences.

Military terminology.

Immediate priorities.

Mechanics taught:

Recognising changed patterns.

Emotional contrast.

Danger inside familiar sounds.

Recognising Vale System member shifts through voice and command style.

End condition:

First major impact interrupts the announcement.

## Part Ten: General Quarters

Time:

Day Seven morning.

Purpose:

End Chapter One and launch Chapter Two.

Soundscape:

Major impact.

Metal twisting.

Alarm tones.

Crew shouting.

Children crying.

Running footsteps.

Pressure doors closing.

Distant decompression.

Captain issuing emergency orders.

Captain Naomi's emergency voice:

Calm.

Controlled.

Urgent.

No longer warm routine.

Player actions:

Limited or none at first.

This moment is primarily emotional.

Chapter end:

General Quarters begins.

Emergency bulkheads close.

Someone calls for Maintenance.

The player hears a person trapped nearby.

Do not end with a traditional mission-complete screen.

Chapter Two should begin immediately and relentlessly.

## Chapter One End State

The player knows Hearth.

The player knows its sounds.

The player knows its people.

The player has performed maintenance.

The player has rescued Mira once.

The player noticed or ignored something wrong.

Now the ship is under attack.

## Chapter Two Setup Rule

Chapter Two begins immediately after Chapter One.

Every primary NPC introduced in Chapter One survives the destruction of Hearth in a separate escape pod or survival craft.

The player must rescue every primary NPC before progressing beyond Chapter Two.

The required rescues are:

Captain Elian of the Vale System.

Doctor Samira Kade.

Maintenance Supervisor Rowan Ives.

Engineer Mara Chen.

Dr. Soren Qadir.

Security Lead Talia Rusk.

Chief Petty Officer Jonah Briggs.

Mira Ives.

Each rescue teaches a core system:

Searching.

Finding objects.

Locating signals.

Docking.

Boarding.

Entering damaged craft.

Rescuing survivors.

Using rescued NPC abilities.

Building a crew.

Every rescued NPC unlocks new capabilities aboard the player's future ship.

People are progression.

Technology alone is never enough.

## Chapter Two Captain Rescue Canon

When the player locates the Captain's escape pod, the first transmission begins:

"This is... the Vale System..."

Static.

Then each member reports in.

"This is Captain Elian of the Vale System."

"This is Captain Naomi of the Vale System."

"This is Captain Gideon of the Vale System."

"This is Captain Iris of the Vale System."

"...reporting from Escape Pod Seven."

The player immediately realises the entire Vale System survived.

Rescuing the Captain becomes one of the defining emotional moments of Chapter Two.
