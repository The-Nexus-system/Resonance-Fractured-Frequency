# Operation One: Exodus — Character Schedule

## Purpose

This document is the definitive scheduling reference for Operation One: Exodus. It answers one question above all others: where is everyone when it happened? It records the ordinary daily routines, favorite places, working responsibilities, relationships, accessibility, sound identities, cross-perspective presence, and emergency behavior of every primary character aboard CSV Hearth during the last ordinary week and on the morning the ship was lost.

The master timeline fixes what happened and when. The Perspective Map, at operation_one_exodus_perspective_map.md, fixes how each profession experiences those same events. This schedule sits between them and fixes who each person is across an ordinary day, so that any campaign can place any character in the right room, doing the right work, for the right reasons.

Where any campaign, scene, or later document disagrees with this schedule about where a character is, what they do, or who they know, this document is canon. It never contradicts the master timeline about the order of events or about Attack Day positions; where the timeline speaks, this schedule obeys it and only adds detail.

## Canon Rules

The following rules govern every use of this schedule. Several restate principles already recorded in the broader canon; here they are applied specifically to character routine and presence. Each rule has its own heading so that no two rules share a label.

### Canon Rule: Characters Live Their Own Lives

Every recurring character lives a complete life whether or not the player is present. They wake, work, eat, rest, and belong on their own schedule. When the player is elsewhere, these routines continue. When the player enters a room, they enter a life already in progress, not a scene waiting to begin.

### Canon Rule: Characters Never Wait for the Player

No character stands idle waiting to be found. No character pauses their duties until the player arrives. If the player reaches a character late, that character has been working the whole time. Presence is a consequence of routine, never a trigger held in reserve.

### Canon Rule: Characters Are Never Quest Dispensers

No character exists solely to hand out tasks. Every request a character makes arises from genuine work they were already doing. A character asks for help because their responsibilities require it, not because the plot needs a familiar face to deliver an objective.

### Canon Rule: Responsibility Comes Before Plot Convenience

Professional responsibility always comes before narrative convenience. No character abandons a post, a patient, a student, or a duty because a scene would be tidier if they did. When a character appears beside the player, it is because their work honestly brought them there.

### Canon Rule: Every Character Has a Complete Routine

Every recurring character has a full daily routine, favorite places, and recurring habits. These are documented here in full, not summarized. A character's routine is dense enough that the player could predict where they are at any hour by knowing who they are.

### Canon Rule: Knowing the Person Reveals the Place

The player should eventually be able to find someone by understanding who they are. If a player knows a character's role, habits, and favorite places, they should be able to guess where that character is at a given time. Understanding a person is a form of navigation.

## How to Read This Document

This document opens with front matter and then gives one section per character. The characters appear in a fixed order: the four members of the Vale System first, in the order Elian, Naomi, Gideon, and Iris, then Rowan Ives, Mara Chen, Doctor Samira Kade, Chief Petty Officer Jonah Briggs, Security Lead Talia Rusk, Dr. Soren Qadir, Mira Ives, and finally Faience.

Every character section uses the same set of disambiguated subsections, each prefixed with the character's name so that no two headings across the whole document are identical. Under each subsection the content is given as bullet lists. The subsections for each character are: Identity and Role; Daily Routine; Professional Responsibilities; Community Relationships; Accessibility; Sound Identity; Cross-Perspective Presence; and Emergency Routine.

Times given here are Ship Standard Time and follow the master timeline. The morning announcement is always at 06:30, the ship quiets by 22:00, and the night shift carries the ship through the dark. On Attack Day the announcement begins at 06:30 and General Quarters is called at 06:35 by Captain Naomi of the Vale System.

A companion document, operation_one_exodus_perspective_map.md, now exists. It describes how each profession experiences the identical events of the week and remains synchronized with this schedule.

## Captain Elian of the Vale System

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Identity and Role

- Identity: Elian, the daily-operations member of the Vale System, the command team who holds the single office of Captain of CSV Hearth.
- Role: Warm daily leadership, morning announcements, crew recognition, and ceremonies.
- Department: Command.
- Rank: Captain, an office shared by the whole Vale System.
- Disability: The Vale System is one person with Dissociative Identity Disorder; Elian is one of four members who front in different contexts.
- Primary workplace: The Bridge and, informally, the Commons, where Elian is most at home among the crew.
- Secondary workplace: The residential corridors and public decks Elian walks to greet residents by name.
- Home neighborhood: The captain's quarters near the command decks, shared by the whole Vale System because they share one body.
- Communication style: Warm, personal, unhurried, remembers names and families, speaks the daily morning announcement in the warm morning-announcement voice.

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Daily Routine

- Wake time: The shared body wakes around 05:45 in the captain's quarters; Elian typically fronts for the morning.
- Morning routine: Reviews the overnight report handed up from the night watch, dresses, and prepares the morning announcement, which is always Elian's.
- Breakfast: Often a quick meal taken while moving toward the Commons, sometimes eaten among the crew rather than alone.
- Work start: Delivers the 06:30 morning announcement; morning shift begins thirty minutes later.
- Morning responsibilities: Shipwide morning address, greeting the first wave in the Commons, and being visibly present as the day begins.
- Lunch: Walks the midday Commons, greeting residents, remembering names and families, and checking that the ship feels like home; eats among the crew.
- Afternoon responsibilities: Ceremonies, recognitions, community visits, and routine shipwide communication; hands more analytical work to Gideon and emergency readiness to Naomi.
- Evening routine: Appears in the gardens and the Commons where there is music, closing the day among the people rather than above them.
- Community activities: Attends evening observances and informal gatherings; welcomes new arrivals personally.
- Night routine: Returns to the captain's quarters as the ship quiets by 22:00; the office passes to the night command watch.
- Sleep: The shared body sleeps in the captain's quarters through the night cycle.
- Preferred routes: From the command decks down through the Commons and along the residential corridors, the longer social route rather than the direct one.
- Favorite quiet place: A garden bed on the agricultural decks where Elian stands to think without being addressed as Captain.
- Favorite public place: The Commons at midday, the warmest and most crowded room on the ship.

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Professional Responsibilities

- Primary duties: The daily morning announcement, daily operations leadership, and maintaining the ship's sense of community.
- Secondary duties: Crew recognition, ceremonies, and welcoming new residents.
- Weekly duties: Presiding over regular community gatherings and the ordinary business of morale.
- Monthly duties: Larger ceremonies and recognitions marking the community's shared calendar.
- Emergency duties: To yield the front to Naomi the moment a crisis becomes an emergency; Elian does not command under fire.
- Who reports to them: The daily-operations staff and the community-facing functions of Command report through the office Elian fronts.
- Who they report to: The office of Captain answers to the community it serves; within the Vale System, Elian defers to Naomi in emergencies and to Gideon in investigation.
- Departments they interact with: All of them, but most often Civilian Services, Education, Hydroponics, and the living departments that make Hearth a home.

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Community Relationships

- Friends: Iris, the diplomacy member who shares the same body and the same love of community; the festival committee members.
- Frequent coworkers: The bridge watch, the Commons staff, and the department heads Elian greets each morning.
- Mentors: The former captains and elders whose recorded wisdom the Vale System still consults.
- Students: New officers learning that leadership is mostly showing up and remembering names.
- Lunch companions: Whoever is in the Commons; Elian rarely eats with the same people twice by design.
- Neighbors: The command-deck residents near the captain's quarters.
- People they naturally check on: New arrivals, grieving families, and anyone whose seat in the Commons has been empty.
- Children who know them: Nearly all of them; Elian is the voice that says good morning to the whole ship, and Mira Ives among them.
- People they quietly admire: Rowan Ives and the maintenance crews who keep the ship alive unthanked, a theme Elian returns to in the announcements.

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Accessibility

- Adaptive equipment: The shipwide announcement system renders Elian's voice as captioned and signed text on every deck as a matter of course.
- Communication methods: Spoken address paired with captions and sign relay so that every resident receives the morning announcement.
- Environmental adaptations: The Commons and command decks are laid out for every body, so Elian's habit of walking the room excludes no one.
- Workstation adaptations: The bridge stations serve the shared body's needs regardless of which member is fronting.
- How they naturally improve accessibility for others: By making the morning announcement a shared, captioned, signed ritual, Elian ensures the whole community starts each day with the same information in the form each person needs.

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Sound Identity

- Voice: Warm, friendly, unhurried, the morning-announcement voice the whole ship knows and wakes to.
- Walking pattern: The shared body's footsteps, the same for every member; Elian tends to walk slowly, stopping often to greet people.
- Equipment sounds: The gentle two-tone morning claxon that precedes every announcement.
- Mobility sounds: Ordinary footfalls on the Commons deck, frequently pausing.
- Tool sounds: None particular; Elian's instrument is the announcement channel.
- Music preferences: The informal evening music of the Commons and the gardens, which Elian seeks out.
- Typical environmental sounds: The crowded, layered warmth of the Commons and the soft green quiet of the gardens.

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Cross-Perspective Presence

- Maintenance: Elian's morning announcement opens the maintenance day, and Elian is the voice that thanks the unseen crews; a maintenance player hears Elian daily and meets Elian in the Commons.
- Engineering: Elian appears in Engineering only as the announcement and as the leadership that trusts Mara's ears; the engineering player knows Elian mostly by voice.
- Medical: Elian visits Medical to recognize its staff and check on recovering residents, part of ordinary community leadership.
- Research: Elian rarely enters the restricted research decks; the research player hears Elian as the shipwide voice rather than a visitor.
- Security: Elian coordinates with Security on ceremonies and crowd-friendly community events, never on threat.
- Command: Elian is the daily face of Command; the command player fronts as Elian for ordinary operations and hands off to the other members as situations demand.
- Logistics: Elian relies on Jonah Briggs to make the community events run and appears wherever Logistics has set the day in motion.
- Civilian: Elian is most present to civilians of all, walking the Commons and the corridors, remembering names, welcoming new families; the civilian player knows Elian best.

### Captain Elian of the Vale System — Emergency Routine

- Where they report: On Attack Day Elian is on the bridge giving the Founding Day announcement at 06:30.
- Who they contact first: The moment the crisis becomes an emergency at 06:32 the announcement stops mid-sentence and the front passes to Naomi; Elian does not command under fire.
- Who they protect: Through the office, everyone, but Elian's role in the emergency is to step aside for the member built for it.
- Who they coordinate with: Elian hands to Naomi and does not front again during the emergency.
- How their routine changes during General Quarters: Elian's routine ends; the warm morning voice is replaced by Naomi's emergency voice at 06:35.
- Where they are when the attack begins: In the captain's chair on the bridge, mid-announcement, when the anomaly resolves at 06:32; the body remains on the bridge as Naomi takes command and launches last among the primary crew.

## Captain Naomi of the Vale System

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Identity and Role

- Identity: Naomi, the emergency-command member of the Vale System, fronting for crises, General Quarters, and drills.
- Role: Emergency command, crisis management, evacuation, and combat leadership.

- Home neighborhood: The captain's quarters near the command decks, shared by the whole Vale System.
- Communication style: Lower, firmer voice; direct; short commands; calm under pressure; military cadence.

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Daily Routine

- Wake time: The shared body wakes around 05:45; on an ordinary day Naomi fronts only briefly, usually to maintain readiness.
- Morning routine: On ordinary days Naomi does not lead the morning; she reviews the emergency-readiness schedule when she fronts.
- Breakfast: Taken as the shared body, usually while Elian or another member is fronting.
- Work start: Naomi's work is triggered by need, not by the clock; on ordinary days her presence is quiet.
- Morning responsibilities: Keeping the emergency-readiness schedule current, confirming drill readiness, and reviewing evacuation routes.
- Lunch: The shared body eats in the Commons; Naomi rarely fronts socially.
- Afternoon responsibilities: Scheduled readiness checks and drills; on Day Five she runs a scheduled readiness check per the timeline.
- Evening routine: On ordinary days Naomi steps back and lets the community-facing members close the day.
- Community activities: Naomi's contribution to community is that the community is safe; she is not the ceremonial face.
- Night routine: The shared body returns to quarters by 22:00; Naomi remains the member who would front instantly if the night went wrong.
- Sleep: The shared body sleeps in the captain's quarters.
- Preferred routes: The most direct routes between the bridge, the coordination center, and the evacuation assembly points.
- Favorite quiet place: The empty drill spaces after a completed drill, where readiness has been confirmed and nothing is wrong.
- Favorite public place: The bridge, which is public in the sense that the ship's safety is a shared thing.

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Professional Responsibilities

- Primary duties: Emergency command, General Quarters, and crisis leadership.
- Secondary duties: Maintaining drill schedules and evacuation readiness.
- Weekly duties: Reviewing and updating the emergency-readiness schedule.
- Monthly duties: Running full-ship drills so that the crew practices in calm what they may one day need under fire.
- Emergency duties: To front instantly and take command the moment a crisis becomes an emergency; to call General Quarters; to direct damage control, triage, evacuation, and defense.
- Who reports to them: In an emergency, every department reports through Naomi.
- Who they report to: The office of Captain; within the Vale System, Naomi holds precedence during emergencies.
- Departments they interact with: Damage Control, Medical, Security, Engineering, and Logistics most closely, and all departments during General Quarters.

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Community Relationships

- Friends: The other members of the Vale System, who trust Naomi to hold the ship together when everything is failing.
- Frequent coworkers: Security Lead Talia Rusk and Chief Petty Officer Jonah Briggs, her closest partners in any emergency.
- Mentors: The emergency-command traditions and former captains whose crisis doctrine the Vale System carries.
- Students: The damage-control and drill teams she trains in calm.
- Lunch companions: The shared body eats with whoever is present; Naomi is not the social front.
- Neighbors: The command-deck residents near the captain's quarters.
- People they naturally check on: The readiness of the crew, the state of the drills, and the members of her own system who will be exhausted by a real emergency.
- Children who know them: Children know Naomi mainly as the firmer voice from the drills, distinct from Elian's morning warmth.
- People they quietly admire: Talia Rusk, whose steadiness under threat matches her own.

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Accessibility

- Adaptive equipment: The emergency systems render Naomi's General Quarters orders as haptic alarms, captions, and sign relay on every deck.
- Communication methods: Short spoken commands paired with captioned and signed emergency instructions so that no resident misses an evacuation order.
- Environmental adaptations: Evacuation routes and assembly points are accessible by design, so Naomi's orders can be obeyed by every body.
- Workstation adaptations: The bridge and coordination-center stations serve the shared body regardless of which member fronts.
- How they naturally improve accessibility for others: By insisting drills be run for every kind of body, Naomi ensures the accessible emergency systems actually work when they are finally needed.

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Sound Identity

- Voice: Lower, firmer, direct; the emergency voice the whole ship recognizes as distinct from Elian's warmth.
- Walking pattern: The shared body's footsteps, faster and more purposeful when Naomi fronts.
- Equipment sounds: The emergency claxon pattern that replaces the gentle morning two-tone chime when she calls General Quarters.
- Mobility sounds: Quick, deliberate footfalls between the bridge and the coordination center.
- Tool sounds: The shipwide emergency channel opening under her voice.
- Music preferences: Naomi does not seek music; her signature sound is command, not melody.
- Typical environmental sounds: Drill claxons, organized radio traffic, and the changed, tense soundscape of a ship under General Quarters.

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Cross-Perspective Presence

- Maintenance: Naomi's General Quarters order sends Rowan into the hidden routes; the maintenance player takes their evacuation-support orders from her voice.
- Engineering: Naomi orders Engineering to hold power and life support long enough for evacuation; Mara works to her command.
- Medical: Naomi orders Medical to triage; Samira runs the bay under that order.
- Research: Naomi's evacuation order tells Soren to preserve what he can and get his people out.
- Security: Naomi and Talia are the closest partners in any crisis; Security runs the routes on Naomi's command.
- Command: The command player fronts as Naomi during any emergency; she is the crisis face of the Vale System.
- Logistics: Naomi's orders become Jonah's moving map of the evacuation; Logistics turns her commands into tracked reality.
- Civilian: Civilians hear Naomi as the firm voice that tells them where to go on the worst morning of their lives; she is the reason they reach the pods.

### Captain Naomi of the Vale System — Emergency Routine

- Where they report: The bridge, instantly, the moment the crisis becomes an emergency.
- Who they contact first: Damage control, medical, security, and logistics, in the short clear orders that open General Quarters.
- Who they protect: Everyone aboard; Naomi's whole purpose is the survival of the community.
- Who they coordinate with: Gideon reads the failing systems for patterns, Iris keeps the civilian channels calm, and Naomi directs the whole evacuation.
- How their routine changes during General Quarters: General Quarters is Naomi's routine; she fronts, calls it at 06:35, and holds command for the duration.
- Where they are when the attack begins: On Attack Day Naomi takes the front at 06:35, calls General Quarters, holds the bridge through the evacuation, and launches last among the primary crew, command staying with the ship until it can no longer be commanded.
