# Community Simulation Bible

This document defines how civilisation lives in Resonance, and it establishes that every non-player character continues living whether or not the player is present. It is the franchise reference for the everyday activity that fills a persistent world, so that a returning player rejoins a community that has kept living rather than reopening a paused game.

## How the living community works

The community simulation is the layer of Resonance that makes a place feel inhabited. It is not scenery and it is not a backdrop for player action; it is a continuous, self-directed civilisation whose residents pursue their own routines, relationships, and projects. This document catalogues the parts of that civilisation and how each behaves, always in service of three canon commitments: the world continues when the player is away, community grows audibly, and accessibility is woven through community life rather than added on top of it.

Every element described below is designed to run without a player watching it. A birth still happens, a garden still ripens, a council still meets, and a memorial is still tended, whether one player, many players, or no players are present. The player is a resident who joins this activity, contributes to it, and leaves it richer, consistent with the canon that the world hosts the game and players are its residents.

### The world continues when the player is away

The simulation never pauses because a player disconnects. While a player is absent, residents keep their schedules, projects advance, research concludes, gardens grow, repairs are carried out, medical care continues, children attend school, and community meetings take place. When the player returns, the game offers a meaningful summary of what changed, so that returning feels like rejoining a community that missed them.

- Life events occur on their own timelines rather than waiting for an audience.
- Long projects advance in the background and can complete during an absence.
- Residents redistribute responsibilities when a member is away, so nothing collapses because one person is missing.
- The returning player always has a meaningful way to contribute rather than feeling left behind.

### Community grows audibly

Because sound is progression, the community makes itself heard before it is seen. A growing settlement gains new ambient layers, new conversations, new work rhythms, and new routines, and the player should notice civilisation returning through their ears first. Every element in this document therefore has an acoustic identity: a birth, a festival, a workshop, a garden, and a memorial each sound distinct, and a resident who cannot see the community can still perceive it thriving.

- New residents and new activities permanently change a place's soundscape.
- Quiet is used deliberately, never merely because a sound has not been authored.
- Acoustic richness precedes visual expansion, so the community is heard growing before it is shown growing.

### Accessibility woven through community life

Accessibility is standard equipment across the whole simulation. No civic activity, celebration, or service depends on a single sense, and no resident is excluded from community life because of disability. Schools teach across differences of body, sense, and language; celebrations are legible by sound, sight, and touch; announcements arrive as speech, text, and tactile signal; and every gathering space is navigable by cane, wheelchair, screen reader, and refreshable braille display. Disability is an ordinary part of the population rather than an exception the community must accommodate reluctantly.

- Every civic space is designed for cane travel, wheelchair use, and tactile navigation by default.
- Every announcement and notice is available as speech, captioned text, and tactile output.
- Community events are designed to be understood fully without sight and without hearing.

## The rhythm of life and remembrance

Residents are born, celebrate the passing of years, grow old, and are remembered. This rhythm gives a persistent world a sense of real time and makes each community feel multi-generational rather than static.

### Births

New residents are born into the community as an ordinary and joyful part of its life. A birth is prepared for by Medical and by families, announced gently to the neighbourhood, and marked by small acts of welcome such as meals brought to a household and a new name added to the community record. Births can occur while a player is away, and the returning player learns of them through the safe-return summary and through the changed daily life around them.

- A birth adds a new resident who will grow, attend school, and eventually contribute in their own right.
- The event is legible without sight or hearing, through announcements delivered in speech, text, and tactile signal.
- The neighbourhood's soundscape gains the presence of a newborn and the quiet routines that surround early care.

### Birthdays

Birthdays are personal milestones the community observes warmly and without spectacle. Friends, families, and colleagues remember the day, and small gatherings, shared meals, and messages mark it. Because residents live on their own timelines, birthdays occur throughout the calendar and continue whether or not the player is present to attend.

- Each resident carries a remembered date that the community honours in ordinary, human ways.
- A birthday may prompt messages left for an absent player by members of the community.
- Celebrations are modest, personal, and shaped by the individual rather than by a fixed template.

### Deaths

Death is part of a living civilisation, and Resonance treats it with dignity rather than avoidance. When a resident dies, the community grieves, gathers, and continues, consistent with the canon that the world continues building even during loss. A death changes relationships, routines, and the soundscape of the places the person frequented, and it is never rendered as a mechanical setback stripped of meaning.

- A death alters the daily lives of those who knew the person and leaves lasting traces in the community's record.
- Grief is written and expressed respectfully, and the community supports the bereaved rather than moving past them.
- The absence is audible: a familiar routine falls quiet, and the community's sound shifts to reflect the loss.

### Memorials

Memorials preserve the memory of those the community has lost and of the events that shaped it. They are tended over time, visited on significant dates, and woven into the Knowledge Archive as historical record. A memorial is a place and a practice, not a single ceremony, and residents continue to care for it when no player is watching.

- Memorials are maintained continuously and are visited on meaningful anniversaries.
- Each memorial is navigable and legible without sight or hearing, with tactile and spoken elements.
- Memory recorded at a memorial connects to the Knowledge Archive so that remembrance changes understanding rather than remaining decorative.

## Families and relationships

The bonds between residents are the true substance of the community. Families, friendships, and partnerships form, deepen, change, and endure, and they drive much of the everyday activity that fills a persistent world.

### Families

Families take many forms in Resonance, and the community treats them all as ordinary. Households share meals, raise children, care for elders, and organise their days together, and community itself is treated as an extension of family. Family life continues independently of any player, with its routines, its small conflicts, and its shared projects.

- Households run their own daily routines of meals, care, rest, and shared work.
- Family structures are diverse and are presented as a normal part of civilisation rather than as exceptions.
- A family's home has its own acoustic identity that changes as the household changes.

### Friendships

Friendships form through proximity, shared work, and shared interests, and they shape where residents spend their time. Friends check on one another, collaborate on projects, and gather in the community's shared spaces. Friendships can form, cool, and rekindle over time, and they persist across a player's absences.

- Residents build friendships through repeated contact in work, study, and leisure.
- Friendships influence who gathers where, and therefore the sound and life of shared spaces.
- Friends may leave messages or hold a place for an absent player, so returning feels like being missed.

### Relationships

Beyond friendship and family, residents form the fuller range of human relationships: mentorships, rivalries reconciled, professional partnerships, and close companionships. These relationships evolve through lived interaction rather than through fixed scripts, and they are recognised by the player through observation, consistent with the canon that characters are known through voice, manner, routine, and how they treat one another.

- Relationships change gradually through ongoing interaction rather than instant events.
- The state of a relationship is legible through behaviour and tone rather than through interface labels.
- Relationships shape whom residents help, confide in, and celebrate with.

### Dating

Residents court and form romantic attachments as an ordinary part of adult life. Dating is portrayed with warmth and respect, without spectacle, and it unfolds at a human pace. It is one of many ways relationships deepen in the community, and it continues in the background of a persistent world.

- Courtship progresses gradually and respectfully, at the pace of the people involved.
- Attraction and partnership are portrayed inclusively and without stereotype.
- New attachments quietly change routines, shared spaces, and the community's social map.

### Marriage

Some partnerships become lasting commitments the community recognises and celebrates. A marriage or comparable union is marked by a gathering that welcomes the whole neighbourhood, and it changes households, living arrangements, and the daily life of those involved. Such commitments are recorded as part of the community's history.

- A union is celebrated communally and recorded in the community's shared history.
- The celebration is designed to be understood fully by sound, sight, and touch.
- A commitment reshapes households and can lead to new family life, including adoption and the raising of children.

### Adoption

Families grow through adoption as readily as through birth, and adopted children belong fully and without qualification. The community treats adoption as an ordinary, joyful route to family, and adoptive households receive the same welcome and support as any other.

- Adoption is presented as a complete and ordinary way to form a family.
- Adopted children attend school, play, and grow up as full members of the community.
- The event is welcomed communally and reflected in the changed life of the household.

## Animals and companionship

Animals live alongside residents, both as companions and as working partners. They are part of the sound and texture of a living community and are cared for as members of it.

### Pets

Residents keep companion animals that share their homes and routines. Pets add warmth, responsibility, and distinctive sound to the community, and they are cared for continuously, including while a player is away, by their households and by neighbours who help.

- Companion animals share the daily routines and spaces of their households.
- Each animal contributes a recognisable acoustic presence to its neighbourhood.
- Care for pets continues during a player's absence, handled by the wider community.

### Service animals

Service animals and guide animals are working partners trained to support the residents they accompany, and they are treated with the respect due to working professionals rather than as pets. They are an ordinary part of accessibility across the community, and public spaces are designed to welcome them.

- Service and guide animals are working partners and are welcome throughout community spaces.
- Their presence is part of accessibility as standard equipment rather than a special accommodation.
- The community understands and respects the difference between a working animal on duty and a companion animal at leisure.

## Learning, memory, and play

Knowledge lives in the community through its schools, libraries, museums, and research, and children learn as much through play as through instruction. These institutions keep the community's memory and carry it forward.

### Schools

Children attend school as an ordinary part of community life, and schooling continues whether or not any player is present. Schools teach across every difference of body, sense, and language, so that no child is excluded, and they are among the clearest expressions of the community's commitment to the next generation.

- Classes run on their own schedule and continue during a player's absence.
- Teaching is multi-modal, supporting spoken, signed, written, braille, tactile, and augmentative and alternative communication.
- The rhythm of the school day gives its part of the community a recognisable daily soundscape.

### Libraries

Libraries hold the community's shared reading and reference materials in fully accessible forms, and they are quiet, welcoming civic spaces. They connect to the community Knowledge Archive so that what residents learn can change how the community acts.

- Every holding is available in accessible formats, including audio, braille, and screen-reader-compatible text.
- Libraries are calm, navigable spaces whose deliberate quiet is an intentional acoustic identity.
- Library holdings connect to the Knowledge Archive, so reading can lead to action rather than to collection alone.

### Museums

Museums preserve and present the community's history, its artefacts, and the legacy it studies, and they are designed so that every exhibit can be understood without sight and without hearing. They are places where memory becomes shared understanding, and they continue to receive and care for their collections over time.

- Exhibits are legible through tactile, spoken, and captioned interpretation, never through one sense alone.
- Museums preserve artefacts and history as stewardship rather than as ownership.
- Collections grow and change over time and are tied to the Knowledge Archive.

### Research

Research continues in the community independently of the player, and it can begin, advance, and conclude during an absence. Research studies the wider universe and the legacy the community protects, and its findings must always change something, consistent with the canon that knowledge produces action rather than remaining lore.

- Research projects run on their own timelines and may conclude while a player is away.
- Concluded research appears in the safe-return summary and changes what the community can do.
- Findings enter the Knowledge Archive and alter dialogue, work, and possibility rather than sitting inert.

### Children's games

Children play throughout the community, inventing games, joining in seasonal pastimes, and drawing adults into their fun. Play is where much of the community's curiosity and joy is audible, and children's games are designed to include every child regardless of body, sense, or communication method.

- Games are inclusive by design, so every child can join in a role that suits them.
- The sound of children playing is a key signal that a community is thriving.
- Play encourages curiosity and exploration rather than competition alone.

## Work, building, and infrastructure

The community sustains and expands itself through work: the volunteering that binds neighbours, the maintenance that keeps a place alive, the construction that grows it, and the utilities that supply it. Major work is done by people together rather than by any individual alone.

### Volunteer work

Much of community life runs on volunteering. Residents give time to help neighbours, support gatherings, tend shared spaces, and assist those who are away, and this generosity is one of the ways communities adapt when members are unavailable. Volunteering is open to every resident, in roles that suit their abilities.

- Residents volunteer for civic tasks, mutual aid, and support of shared events.
- Volunteering absorbs the responsibilities of absent members so nothing falls apart.
- Roles are offered in accessible forms, so every resident can contribute meaningfully.

### Maintenance schedules

Maintenance keeps the community alive, and it follows regular schedules that run continuously in the background. Repairs are carried out, systems are inspected, and preventative work is done whether or not a player is watching, and the disappearance of a fault is often heard before it is seen.

- Scheduled inspection and repair continue during a player's absence.
- Resolved faults change the soundscape, as leaks and instabilities give way to steady operation.
- Maintenance is a shared responsibility connected to the wider crew and community.

### Construction

The community grows through construction, and major building projects require several residents with different expertise rather than one person with enough resources. Construction advances over real time, can complete while a player is away, and permanently changes both the shape and the sound of a place.

- Large projects require cooperation across multiple skills and cannot be completed alone.
- Construction advances in the background and can finish during an absence.
- A completed project changes a location's layout, accessibility, and acoustic identity.

### Utilities

Utilities are the essential services that keep the community habitable, including power, water, air, heating, lighting, and communication. They are maintained continuously, prioritised in emergencies, and designed so that accessibility and essential communication are protected first.

- Essential services run and are maintained continuously, whether or not a player is present.
- Utility status is communicated in accessible forms and prioritised during disruption.
- Steady utilities have a quiet, dependable acoustic identity that signals a place in good health.

## Governance and gathering

Residents share responsibility for the places they live, and they gather to decide, to worship, and to organise. Governance in the community is cooperative and ongoing rather than centred on any single leader or player.

### Neighbourhood councils

Neighbourhoods govern much of their own daily life through councils in which residents share responsibility for their shared spaces. Councils meet on their own schedule, make decisions about local matters, and continue their work when individual members, including the player, are away.

- Councils meet regularly and decide local matters through shared responsibility.
- Council business continues during a player's absence and is summarised on return.
- Meetings are held in accessible forms so every resident can take part.

### Community meetings

Beyond neighbourhood councils, the wider community gathers for meetings that share news, plan projects, and mark decisions that affect everyone. These meetings happen whether or not a player attends, and their outcomes appear in the safe-return summary.

- Community meetings occur on their own schedule and do not wait for the player.
- Outcomes and announcements are recorded and included in the returning player's summary.
- Every meeting is captioned, spoken, and tactilely accessible so no resident is excluded.

### Religious gatherings

Residents hold spiritual and religious gatherings according to their own traditions, and the community makes room for many beliefs and practices as an ordinary part of civilisation. These gatherings are portrayed respectfully and inclusively, and they are designed to be accessible to every participant.

- The community supports a plurality of beliefs and practices without privileging any one as default.
- Gatherings are portrayed with respect and without stereotype.
- Participation is fully accessible, so belief is never gated behind a single sense or ability.

## Food, gardens, and exchange

The community feeds itself through gardens, kitchens, and shared meals, and it exchanges goods and labour toward common projects. Food is both sustenance and a communal ritual at the heart of daily life.

### Gardens

Gardens grow continuously, and they are among the clearest signs that the world continues when a player is away. They feed the community, offer restful shared spaces, and change with the seasons, and their tending is a daily, cooperative practice.

- Gardens keep growing during a player's absence and may be ready to harvest on return.
- Tending is a shared routine that gives gardens a distinctive, restful soundscape.
- Gardens are navigable and legible by touch, sound, and sight, so every resident can take part.

### Food distribution

The community shares food rather than accumulating it privately, distributing what its gardens and kitchens produce so that everyone is fed. Distribution runs as an ordinary civic routine and adapts to ensure no resident goes without, including those who are away or unable to collect for themselves.

- Food is shared toward common need rather than individual accumulation.
- Distribution continues as a routine and adapts to cover absent or unavailable residents.
- The logistics of distribution are accessible to every resident, as giver and as receiver.

### Restaurants

Shared kitchens and eating places are social hearts of the community, where residents gather over meals, celebrate, and simply spend time together. They have lively, recognisable soundscapes and are designed to welcome every resident.

- Eating places are social spaces as much as sources of food.
- Each has a warm, recognisable acoustic identity that signals gathering and welcome.
- Menus, ordering, and seating are accessible, so dining excludes no one.

### Economic activity

The community's economy is built on shared contribution rather than personal accumulation, and residents give labour, skill, and produce toward common projects. Economic life continues as an ordinary background activity, and its purpose is to sustain community rather than to enrich individuals.

- Residents contribute labour and skill toward shared projects rather than private wealth.
- Economic activity continues in the background whether or not a player participates.
- Contribution is possible for every resident in a form suited to their abilities.

### Trade

The community exchanges goods, knowledge, and help both within itself and, where contact allows, with others, always as stewardship rather than exploitation. Trade supports the community's projects and relationships, and it is conducted with consent and respect.

- Trade circulates goods, knowledge, and assistance in support of shared goals.
- Exchange is conducted respectfully and by consent, consistent with stewardship over acquisition.
- Trade continues as part of ordinary community life and is accessible to every participant.

## Culture and celebration

The community expresses itself through festivals, music, sport, and the celebrations that follow the turning seasons. Culture is how a community remembers who it is and welcomes newcomers into that shared identity.

### Festivals

Festivals bring the whole community together to mark meaningful occasions, including the founding the community counts toward together. They are among the most vivid expressions of shared life, and they occur on the calendar whether or not a player attends.

- Festivals occur on their own schedule and continue during a player's absence.
- Each festival is designed to be experienced fully by sound, sight, and touch.
- Festivals welcome newcomers and strengthen the community's shared identity.

### Seasonal celebrations

The community observes the turning of its seasons and calendar with recurring celebrations that give the year its shape. These celebrations return dependably, connect to gardens and food, and mark the passage of time in a persistent world.

- Seasonal observances recur across the calendar and mark the passage of real time.
- They connect to gardens, harvests, and shared meals as the year turns.
- Every observance is accessible and legible without reliance on a single sense.

### Music

Music runs through the whole of community life, from private practice to public performance, and it is a form of memory, communication, and celebration as well as art. Because sound is gameplay in Resonance, music is a first-class part of the community's identity, and it is presented so that it can be perceived and appreciated in multiple ways.

- Music appears throughout daily life, in practice, gathering, celebration, and mourning.
- It carries memory and meaning as well as pleasure, consistent with sound as identity.
- Music is presented with visual and tactile equivalents so it can be shared by every resident.

### Sports

Residents play and follow sports and games of skill as recreation and as community bonding. Sport in Resonance rewards participation and cooperation over spectacle, and every activity is designed with inclusive variants so that residents of every ability can take part.

- Sport is recreational and communal rather than a contest of exclusion.
- Inclusive variants ensure every resident can participate in a role that suits them.
- The sounds of play and support are part of a thriving community's acoustic life.

## Resilience and recovery

A living community also endures hardship, and how it recovers is part of who it is. Resonance shows a community that rebuilds together and comes out stronger, consistent with the canon that humanity keeps building even through loss.

### Disaster recovery

When the community suffers damage or loss, it organises to recover, repair, care for the affected, and rebuild. Recovery is cooperative work that draws on many residents and many kinds of expertise, and it leaves the community changed rather than simply restored. A player's absence must never become the cause of catastrophic failure, and recovery continues whether or not any individual player is present.

- Recovery is cooperative, drawing on maintenance, medical, volunteer, and council efforts together.
- The community adapts and rebuilds so that it emerges changed and, wherever possible, better.
- Recovery continues during a player's absence and never depends on a single resident to succeed.
