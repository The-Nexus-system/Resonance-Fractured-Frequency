# Crew Progression System

This document is the canonical, franchise-wide reference for how the player's crew grows throughout Resonance. Every chapter, campaign, gameplay system, accessibility system and future expansion should reference this document when determining what changes after a crew member joins.

Several rules restated here also appear in the master Canon Rules document. That document holds precedence for the wording of a rule; the canon rule sections below elaborate those rules specifically for crew progression rather than replacing them.

## Purpose

This document defines how people—not equipment—drive progression throughout Resonance.

The player does not become stronger simply by collecting resources.

The player becomes stronger because the crew grows.

Every rescued crew member permanently changes:

- Gameplay
- Available mechanics
- Knowledge
- Conversations
- Ambient sound
- Accessibility
- Available missions
- Problem-solving approaches
- The emotional state of the player's home

Community is progression.

## Canon Philosophy

Technology is important.

People are essential.

Technology alone never solves the game's largest problems.

The player succeeds by building relationships, trusting expertise and creating an adaptive community.

The station becomes more capable because its people become more capable together.

## Canon Rule: Portraits Represent Belonging

A rescued crew member is not considered "home" immediately after rescue.

A crew member becomes home only after:

- They unpack.
- They settle.
- They begin contributing.
- The environment changes because of their presence.

Only after these changes occur does their portrait illuminate.

Portraits do not represent rescue.

Portraits represent belonging.

## Canon Rule: Sound Is Progression

Every rescued crew member permanently changes the station's soundscape.

The player should hear the community growing before they ever see visual confirmation.

Sound is progression.

Visual confirmation follows sound.

## Canon Rule: What Every Crew Member Unlocks

Every rescued crew member permanently unlocks:

- At least one major gameplay system.
- At least one new environment.
- At least one new conversation category.
- At least one Knowledge Archive topic.
- At least one future mission opportunity.
- At least one accessibility improvement.
- At least one long-term upgrade path.

## Canon Rule: Crew Members Stay Relevant

Every primary crew member must remain mechanically and emotionally relevant throughout the entire campaign.

No primary crew member exists only to unlock a system and then become background decoration.

Every chapter should provide meaningful opportunities for every crew member to contribute according to their expertise.

As the station grows, the community grows with it.

People remain progression from the first rescue to the final chapter.

## Sound Progression

The station should never become visually larger before it becomes acoustically richer.

Every rescue adds:

- New ambient sounds
- New conversations
- New work
- New routines
- New life

The player should hear civilization returning.

## Knowledge Progression

Knowledge is progression.

Research is optional.

Ignoring research never prevents finishing the story.

Ignoring research increases difficulty.

Knowledge unlocks:

- Dialogue
- Negotiation
- Alternative solutions
- Historical understanding
- Improved accessibility
- Diplomatic options
- Scientific discoveries
- Reduced combat difficulty

The game rewards curiosity rather than forcing it.

## Accessibility Progression

Accessibility is not a settings menu.

Accessibility is part of civilization.

As the crew grows, accessibility options naturally expand because different people contribute different technologies, experiences and ideas.

The player's home becomes more accessible over time because community builds accessibility together.

## Franchise Rule

Every future crew member introduced in Resonance must answer three questions:

- What gameplay do they unlock?
- How do they change the soundscape?
- How do they expand the player's understanding of community?

If those questions cannot be answered, the character is not yet ready to join the crew.
