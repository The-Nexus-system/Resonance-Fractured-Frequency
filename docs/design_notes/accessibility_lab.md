# Accessibility Lab

This is a design note, not yet canon. The Accessibility Lab is a space to prototype emerging accessibility technology and new interaction methods without cluttering the core game design. Ideas here graduate into the Accessibility Bible or Systems Bible once they mature.

## Purpose

The Accessibility Lab lets the team explore ambitious accessibility ideas early, experiment freely, and keep the core documents stable. It exists so that no promising idea is lost while it is still forming.

## Areas to prototype

- Braille display support, including navigation and command bindings.
- Tactile music, where musical information is expressed through touch and haptics.
- Wearable haptics for directional and status information.
- Smart canes and other connected mobility aids.
- Switch devices and other alternative input hardware.
- New interaction methods that do not fit existing gesture or binding models.

## How ideas graduate

1. Prototype and evaluate an idea here.
2. When it is proven and approved, write it into the Accessibility Bible or Systems Bible as appropriate.
3. Record the decision in the Decision Log.
4. Remove or update the note here so there is only one living definition.
