# Campaign Bible

This document defines the franchise structure, the first campaign, the perspective system, and the chapters of the opening operation.

## Franchise

The franchise is Resonance.

## Campaign One

The first campaign is Resonance: Fractured Frequency.

## Perspective system

The player does not choose a class. The player chooses a profession. Every profession experiences the same historical events from a different perspective.

Available professions include the following.

- Medic
- Scientist
- Engineer
- Maintenance
- Security
- Commissioned Officer
- Non-Commissioned Officer
- Civilian

Every profession changes the experience across many dimensions.

- Starting location
- Objectives
- Professional vocabulary
- Relationships
- Dialogue
- Knowledge
- AI communication
- Environmental awareness

Replayability comes from perspective rather than from alternate timelines. The events are the same; how the player lives through them is not. The audio dimension of the perspective system is detailed in the Audio Bible.

## Operation One: Exodus

Operation One is called Exodus. It contains seven chapters. Every chapter teaches one major gameplay mechanic while advancing the story.

### Chapter One

Chapter One introduces ordinary life aboard the colony ship from the chosen profession. It establishes the character's routine, relationships, and professional world before anything goes wrong.

### Later chapters

Later chapters progressively introduce new systems while advancing the story. Systems introduced across later chapters include the following.

- The boarding attack
- Evacuation
- Faience
- The damaged helmet
- Navigation through sound
- Escape
- Escape pod combat

The shape of the later chapters follows the chapter question spine below. Detailed, part-by-part design for individual chapters lives in the design notes.

## Chapter design rule

Every chapter follows the same discipline so that gameplay and story grow together.

- Every chapter must introduce one major gameplay system.
- Every chapter must answer one major narrative question.
- Every chapter must end by asking a larger question than it answered.
- Gameplay progression and narrative progression must grow together.
- The player should feel they are becoming more capable for the same reason the story is becoming more complex.

## Chapter question spine

Every chapter begins with a question and ends by asking a larger one. The progression grows from personal survival to civilization and gives the first campaign its narrative spine. The questions for Operation One are the following.

- Chapter One: Can I save my home? The answer is no.
- Chapter Two: Can I find my people? The answer is yes.
- Chapter Three: Can we survive together?
- Chapter Four: Can we build something worth protecting?
- Chapter Five: Who were the Shawabti, really?
- Chapter Six: Why did they choose humanity?
- Chapter Seven: What kind of civilization will we become?

## Emotional pillars

Every major mission should reinforce at least one of these emotional pillars.

- Wonder
- Isolation
- Trust
- Discovery
- Responsibility
- Adaptation
- Belonging
- Curiosity
