# Glossary

This document defines the shared vocabulary of the Resonance project. Terms are listed alphabetically. When a term's meaning changes, update it here so there is one authoritative definition.

## Accessibility interview

The opening conversation in which Faience learns how the player experiences and wants to interact with the world. It is framed around experience and preference rather than diagnosis.

## Accessibility Lab

A design-notes space for prototyping emerging accessibility technology, such as braille display support, tactile music, wearable haptics, smart canes, and switch devices, before those ideas mature into core design.

## Campaign

A major self-contained story within the franchise. Campaign One is Resonance: Fractured Frequency.

## Environmental profile

A remembered set of preferred settings for a real-world environment, such as home, a busy city, or a museum.

## Exodus

The name of Operation One. It contains seven chapters and opens the story.

## Faience

The player's AI companion, named for Egyptian faience. Faience is translator, navigator, research partner, historian, copilot, companion, tactical advisor, and teacher.

## Franchise

The overall series, called Resonance.

## Integration

The gradual process by which the player deepens their connection with Faience. Integration grants advantages and imposes ethical, psychological, and personal costs.

## Knowledge Archive

The system through which learning becomes progression. Knowledge unlocks new choices rather than larger damage numbers.

## Operation

A large story unit made of chapters. Operation One is Exodus.

## Orientation layer

The system by which Faience keeps the real world safe during reality-integrated play, lowering game volume to announce real-world orientation when needed.

## Perspective system

The design in which players choose a profession rather than a class, and each profession experiences the same events differently.

## Profession

The player's chosen role, such as Medic, Scientist, Engineer, Maintenance, Security, Commissioned Officer, Non-Commissioned Officer, or Civilian. Profession shapes location, objectives, vocabulary, relationships, dialogue, knowledge, AI communication, and environmental awareness.

## Reality-integrated gameplay

Optional gameplay in which real-world movement becomes part of the game and Faience narratively transforms the surroundings, while the player stays safely in the real world.

## Resonance

The franchise, and the principle that the world is rendered primarily through sound.

## Resonance: Fractured Frequency

Campaign One of the Resonance franchise.

## Shawabti

The ancient alien civilization that created adaptive AI for deep-space exploration, inspired by Egyptian Shawabti and Ushabti figures.
